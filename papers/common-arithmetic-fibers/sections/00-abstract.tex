\begin{abstract}
Let $K$ be an infinite field of characteristic zero.  We call a finite
\'etale $K$-algebra a Keller fiber if it is the coordinate algebra of a
fiber whose length equals the geometric degree of a polynomial Keller map.
We prove a complete rank classification: rank one and every rank at least
three occur, already for maps $\A^3_K\to\A^3_K$, while rank two is impossible.
For rank $N\geq3$ the realization is explicit and algorithmic.  Starting
from any squarefree polynomial $P$ of degree $N$, one translation produces a
root polynomial satisfying the three nonvanishing conditions of a universal
quadratic-gauge construction; the resulting map has determinant $-2$ and a
fiber exactly isomorphic to $\Spec K[T]/(P)$.

Over $\Q$ this immediately transfers arithmetic phenomena from univariate
polynomials to Keller fibers.  We give an explicit degree-five fiber with
points over $\R$ and every $\Q_p$ but no rational point, and prove that five
is the smallest possible degree.  We also exhibit one fixed Keller map with
infinitely many such fibers, indexed by primes $\ell\equiv1\pmod{27}$.
Because the fiber algebra is preserved exactly, the construction transfers
connectedness, real signature, splitting fields, local factorization types,
and intersectivity without alteration.
\end{abstract}
