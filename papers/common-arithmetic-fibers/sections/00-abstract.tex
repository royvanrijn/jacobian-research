\begin{abstract}
A \emph{Keller fiber} is the coordinate algebra of a full fiber of a
polynomial map with nonzero constant Jacobian, where full means that the
fiber length equals the geometric degree.  Over every field $K$ of
characteristic zero, we classify these algebras by rank: rank one and every
rank at least three occur, already for maps $\A^3_K\to\A^3_K$, while rank two
is impossible.  The realization is explicit and effective.  Given any
squarefree polynomial $P$ of degree $N\geq3$, choose a translation parameter
outside the finite zero set of $P'P'''$.  A universal quadratic-gauge formula,
followed by a fixed linear output normalization, gives a Jacobian-one map of
coordinate degree at most $6N+2$ with a fiber exactly isomorphic to
$\Spec K[T]/(P)$.

Thus every number field of degree at least three occurs as a connected
Keller fiber, the construction commutes with extension of the ground field,
and arithmetic properties of arbitrary finite \'etale algebras transfer
without alteration.  Over $\Q$ we give an explicit degree-five fiber with
points over $\R$ and every $\Q_p$ but no rational point, and prove that five
is the smallest possible degree.  We also exhibit one fixed Keller map with
infinitely many such fibers, indexed by primes $\ell\equiv1\pmod{27}$.
\end{abstract}
