\section{Keller fibers and the rank theorem}

Let $K$ be a field of characteristic zero.  A polynomial map
\[
 F:\A^m_K\longrightarrow\A^m_K
\]
is a \emph{Keller map} if $\det DF\in K^*$.  Its geometric degree is
\[
 \gdeg(F)=[K(x_1,\ldots,x_m):K(F_1,\ldots,F_m)].
\]
A Keller map is \'etale and hence locally quasi-finite.  Every fiber over a
$K$-point is therefore a finite \'etale $K$-scheme.

\begin{definition}[Keller fiber]\label{def:keller-fiber}
A nonzero finite \'etale $K$-algebra $A$ is a \emph{Keller fiber} if there
are a Keller map $F:\A^m_K\to\A^m_K$ and a point $y\in\A^m(K)$ such that
\[
 F^{-1}(y)\simeq\Spec A,
 \qquad \dim_KA=\gdeg(F).
\]
The equality says that the fiber contains every generic inverse sheet; no
sheet is lost at the normalization boundary.
\end{definition}

The construction can be summarized in one line:
\[
 \boxed{
 \text{squarefree }P(T)
 \ \longmapsto\
 G(S)=P(a+S)-P(a)
 \ \longmapsto\
 F_G^{-1}(y_{P,a})\simeq\Spec K[T]/(P).}
\]

The paper is organized around one transfer theorem.

\begin{theorem}[Polynomial-to-fiber transfer]\label{thm:main}
Let $K$ be a field of characteristic zero, and let $P\in K[T]$ be squarefree
of degree $N\geq3$.  Choose $a\in K$ such that
\[
 P'(a)P'''(a)\neq0,
\]
and put
\[
 G(S)=P(a+S)-P(a).
\]
The explicit polynomial map $F_G:\A^3_K\to\A^3_K$ defined in
\eqref{eq:gauge-map} satisfies
\[
 \det DF_G=-2,
 \qquad \gdeg(F_G)=N,
\]
and at
\begin{equation}
 y_{P,a}=\left(1,0,-\frac{2P(a)}{P'(a)}\right)
 \label{eq:main-target}
\end{equation}
its complete fiber is
\[
 \boxed{F_G^{-1}(y_{P,a})\simeq\Spec K[T]/(P).}
\]

Consequently a nonzero finite \'etale $K$-algebra is a Keller fiber if and
only if its rank is not two.  The possible ranks are exactly
\[
 \boxed{1,3,4,5,\ldots}.
\]
\end{theorem}

The hypotheses are sharp for the stated classification.  Squarefreeness is
necessary because a Keller fiber is \'etale.  Rank one is realized by the
identity.  Rank two is impossible: a Keller map of geometric degree two
induces a separable quadratic, hence Galois, function-field extension, and
the Galois case of the Jacobian theorem makes the map a polynomial
automorphism \cite{Campbell,Razar,Wright}.  For $N\geq3$, the product
$P'P'''$ is a nonzero polynomial, so the required $a$ exists automatically.
