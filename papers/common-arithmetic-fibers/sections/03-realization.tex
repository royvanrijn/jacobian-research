\section{Realizing an arbitrary finite \'etale algebra}\label{sec:realization}

We first recall a standard elementary fact in a form that applies to products
of fields as well as to fields.

\begin{lemma}[Monogenicity]\label{lem:monogenic}
Every finite \'etale algebra over an infinite field is generated by one
element.  Equivalently, if $A$ has rank $N$, then
\[
 A\simeq K[T]/(P)
\]
for a squarefree polynomial $P\in K[T]$ of degree $N$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For $\theta\in A$, let $\chi_\theta(T)$ be the characteristic polynomial of
multiplication by $\theta$ on the $K$-vector space $A$, and let
\[
 \Delta(\theta)=\operatorname{disc}(\chi_\theta).
\]
This is a polynomial function on the affine space underlying $A$, defined
over $K$.  After extension to a separable closure, identify
$A\otimes_KK^{\rm sep}$ with $(K^{\rm sep})^N$.  In the resulting coordinates,
\[
 \Delta(\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_N)
 =\prod_{i<j}(\theta_i-\theta_j)^2,
\]
so $\Delta$ is not the zero polynomial.  Since $K$ is infinite, there is
$\theta\in A$ with $\Delta(\theta)\neq0$.  Then $\chi_\theta$ has $N$ distinct
geometric roots, so the minimal polynomial of $\theta$ has degree $N$.
Consequently $K[\theta]=A$, and its minimal polynomial is the required
squarefree polynomial $P$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Compatibility with extension of scalars]
\label{prop:base-change}
Let $K\hookrightarrow K'$ be an extension of characteristic-zero fields.
For a squarefree $P\in K[T]$ and an admissible translation parameter $a\in K$,
the map $\widetilde F_G$ of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}, after coefficientwise
extension to $K'$, is exactly the map obtained by applying the same construction
to $P$ over $K'$.  Its distinguished fiber satisfies
\[
 \widetilde F_{G,K'}^{-1}(\widetilde y_{P,a})
 \simeq
 \Spec\bigl((K[T]/(P))\otimes_KK'\bigr)
 \simeq
 \Spec K'[T]/(P).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The formulas \eqref{eq:tq}--\eqref{eq:gauge-map}, the linear normalization
$L$, and the target \eqref{eq:main-target} use only field operations on the
coefficients of $P$ and the chosen element $a$.  They therefore commute with
coefficientwise scalar extension.  The displayed fiber identity is the usual
base-change isomorphism for a polynomial quotient, and
Proposition~\ref{prop:gauge} identifies it with the full scheme fiber.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Number fields as connected Keller fibers]\label{cor:fields}
Every finite separable field extension $L/K$ of degree at least three occurs
as a connected full fiber of an explicit Jacobian-one polynomial map
$\A^3_K\to\A^3_K$ of coordinate degree at most $6[L:K]+2$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Choose a primitive element of $L/K$ and apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:main} to its separable minimal polynomial.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Arithmetic data are preserved]\label{cor:transfer}
Let $A$ be a finite \'etale algebra of rank at least three over a number
field.  The realization supplied by Theorem~\ref{thm:main} preserves the
algebra $A$ itself.  In particular it preserves:
\begin{itemize}
\item connectedness and field decomposition;
\item real signature;
\item splitting field and Galois action on geometric points;
\item the local algebras $A\otimes_KK_v$ at every place;
\item factorization types at every good prime;
\item the existence or nonexistence of rational and local points.
\end{itemize}
Thus any arithmetic construction of a finite \'etale algebra of rank at
least three transfers verbatim to a Keller fiber.  Proposition~\ref{prop:base-change}
makes the assertions about local algebras and splitting data functorial.
\end{corollary}
