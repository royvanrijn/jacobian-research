\section{Realizing an arbitrary finite \'etale algebra}\label{sec:realization}

We first recall a standard elementary fact.

\begin{lemma}[Monogenicity]\label{lem:monogenic}
Every finite \'etale algebra over an infinite field is generated by one
element.  Equivalently, if $A$ has rank $N$, then
\[
 A\simeq K[T]/(P)
\]
for a squarefree polynomial $P\in K[T]$ of degree $N$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
After extending scalars to a separable closure,
$A\otimes_KK^{\mathrm{sep}}\simeq(K^{\mathrm{sep}})^N$.  An element generates
this product exactly when its $N$ coordinates are pairwise distinct.  The
non-generators are therefore cut out by the discriminant polynomial
\[
 \prod_{i<j}(X_i-X_j)^2,
\]
which is not identically zero.  Since $K$ is infinite, the corresponding
nonempty Zariski-open subset of the affine space underlying $A$ has a
$K$-rational point.  Its characteristic polynomial is the required $P$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
Let $P$ and $a$ be as in the theorem and write
\[
 G(S)=P(a+S)-P(a)=\sum_{i=1}^Ng_iS^i.
\]
Then
\[
 g_1=P'(a),\qquad g_3=\frac{P'''(a)}6,
\]
so $g_1g_3g_N\neq0$.  At the target \eqref{eq:main-target}, the inverse
polynomial \eqref{eq:inverse} is
\[
 G(S)-\frac{g_1}{2}\left(-\frac{2P(a)}{g_1}\right)=P(a+S).
\]
Translation identifies $K[S]/(P(a+S))$ with $K[T]/(P(T))$.
Proposition~\ref{prop:gauge} now gives the displayed full fiber, constant
Jacobian, and geometric degree.

For an arbitrary finite \'etale algebra of rank at least three, apply
Lemma~\ref{lem:monogenic}.  The rank-one and rank-two cases were handled
after the statement of the theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Number fields as connected Keller fibers]\label{cor:fields}
Every finite separable field extension $L/K$ of degree at least three occurs
as a connected full fiber of an explicit Keller map
$\A^3_K\to\A^3_K$ with determinant $-2$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Arithmetic data are preserved]\label{cor:transfer}
Let $A$ be a finite \'etale algebra of rank at least three over a number
field.  The realization supplied by Theorem~\ref{thm:main} preserves the
algebra $A$ itself.  In particular it preserves:
\begin{itemize}
\item connectedness and field decomposition;
\item real signature;
\item splitting field and Galois action on geometric points;
\item the local algebras $A\otimes_KK_v$ at every place;
\item factorization types at every good prime;
\item the existence or nonexistence of rational and local points.
\end{itemize}
Thus any arithmetic construction of a finite \'etale algebra of rank at
least three transfers verbatim to a Keller fiber.
\end{corollary}
