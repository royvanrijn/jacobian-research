\section{Refined cancellation boundary geometry}

This supplemental appendix is deliberately not included in the proof of the
degreewise multiplicity theorem.  That theorem uses only the complete
codimension-one boundary list and the unconditional thick-contact formula
$\mu=mr(m+1)$.  The finer question considered here is how the individual
projective branches above $P=0$ meet one another and the critical prime.

Put
\[
 K_{m,r}(w)=w^{-r-1}\int_0^w v^r(1-v)^{mr}\,dv,
 \qquad p(w)=w(1-w)^m,
\]
\[
 L_{m,r}(w)=w^{-r-1}\int_0^w\{p(w)-p(v)\}^r\,dv.
\]
On the normalized contraction, the critical prime restricts to
\[
 Q^mL_{m,r}(w)
 \quad\text{in}\quad
 \C(R)[w]/(K_{m,r})[[Q]].
\]
Consequently the additional hypothesis
\[
 \operatorname{Res}_w(K_{m,r},L_{m,r})\ne0
\]
makes $L_{m,r}$ a unit in the reduced branch algebra.  After strict
henselization, each geometric $K_{m,r}$-branch then meets the critical prime
in $\C(R)[[Q]]/(Q^m)$; distinct $K$-branches meet in the reduced stratum
$P=Q=0$.  This yields the full pairwise and higher intersection diagram, but
is strictly finer than the total contact length needed by M1.

The endpoint-moment reduction, the uniform proofs for $r=1,2,3,4$, the
bounded direct-resultant certificates, and the remaining conditional ranges
are maintained in
\href{../../cancellation/CONTACT_RESULTANT.md}{Cancellation contact
resultant}.  Their executable certificates are
\path{scripts/verify_contact_resultant_endpoint_reduction.py},
\path{scripts/verify_contact_resultant_r4.py}, and the related focused
resultant scripts.  None is a dependency of the degreewise theorem.
