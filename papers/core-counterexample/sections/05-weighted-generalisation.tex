\section{Weighted generalisation}

Let $H\in k[W]$ have degree $n\ge3$ and satisfy
\[
H(0)=H(1)=0,\quad H'(0)=0,\quad H'(1)=-c\ne0,
\quad H''(1)/c\ne-2.
\]
The weighted construction produces a polynomial Keller map
$G_H:\A^3\to\A^3$ with inverse pencil
\[
E_{A,B,C}(W)=H(W)-BCW+cAC^2.
\]

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:weighted}
The normalization of the finite marked-root incidence $E=0$ contains an open
regular-reconstruction locus isomorphic to $\A^3$.  Under this isomorphism,
$G_H$ is the restriction of the finite degree-$n$ root projection.  Over
$C\ne0$, the affine source is exactly the simple-root locus.
\end{theorem}

The normalization is necessary over $C=0$: a repeated specialized root can
still reconstruct to a finite point, while a simple specialized root can lie
at infinity.  The coordinate-free statement is therefore regularity of all
reconstruction functions on the normalized incidence, not a global
identification with the raw simple-root locus.
