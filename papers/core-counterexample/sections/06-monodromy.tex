\section{Monodromy}

Consider the universal pencil
\[
H(W)-sW+t.
\]
It is geometrically irreducible over $k(s,t)$ by Gauss's lemma and linearity
in $t$.  Its repeated-root discriminant is irreducible and has birational
normalization
\[
r\longmapsto\bigl(H'(r),\,rH'(r)-H(r)\bigr).
\]
The two coordinates have coprime pole orders $n-1$ and $n$, proving generic
degree one.  Generically there is a unique double root, so inertia is a
transposition.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:monodromy}
The geometric and arithmetic monodromy groups of the degree-$n$ universal
pencil are both $S_n$.
\end{theorem}

Geometric irreducibility gives transitivity.  The normal subgroup generated
by codimension-one inertia has unramified fixed cover over affine two-space;
purity and the triviality of finite etale covers of affine space force that
fixed cover to be trivial.  Thus the group is generated by transpositions,
and a transitive group generated by transpositions is symmetric.  We use
Zariski--Nagata purity in the form recorded by the Stacks Project
\cite{stacks-purity} and the characteristic-zero etale/topological comparison
from SGA~1 \cite{sga1}.
