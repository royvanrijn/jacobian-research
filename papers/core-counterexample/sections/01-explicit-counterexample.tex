\section{Explicit counterexample}

Let $k$ be a characteristic-zero field, put $u=1+xy$, and define
$F=(F_1,F_2,F_3):\A_k^3\to\A_k^3$ by
\begin{align*}
F_1&=u^3z+y^2u(4+3xy),\\
F_2&=y+3xu^2z+3xy^2(4+3xy),\\
F_3&=2x-3x^2y-x^3z.
\end{align*}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:counterexample}
The map $F$ has $\det DF=-2$ and
\[
F(0,0,-1/4)=F(1,-3/2,13/2)=F(-1,3/2,13/2)=(-1/4,0,0).
\]
In particular, $F$ is an everywhere-etale noninjective polynomial map.
After adjoining identity coordinates, the same conclusion holds in every
dimension at least three.
\end{theorem}

The proof is split into a structural determinant calculation and exact
substitution.  This separation makes the finite certificate easy to reproduce
without relying on the later geometry.
