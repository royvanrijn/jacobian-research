\section{Further directions}

The marked-root viewpoint suggests two directions.  First, one can study the
boundary of the normalized inverse incidence: its divisorial valuations
control nonproperness, sheet loss, and reconstruction poles.  Second, one can
seek other cancellation mechanisms whose inverse problem is governed by a
one-variable primitive element.

The repository's only active research branch is the latter question for the
master cancellation family C24.  The immediate goal is to construct a
coordinate-independent invariant of the canonical finite normalization that
obstructs polynomial left--right equivalence.  This paper does not depend on
that programme.  The more extensive discriminant, transfer-block, arithmetic,
and compactification notes are retained as experimental work awaiting review
and are intentionally not summarized here.
