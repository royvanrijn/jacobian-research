\section{Subsequent developments}\label{sec:further}

Later repository papers develop the inverse geometry beyond the scope of the
present foundational article.  The exact image, nonproperness set, and
special fibers of the weighted family are treated in
\texttt{extended-geometry/CANONICAL\_FAMILY\_IMAGE.md} and
\texttt{verified/IMAGE\_AND\_NONPROPERNESS.md}.  The normalized-cover and
boundary formalism is developed in
\texttt{MARKED\_ROOT\_KELLER\_MAPS.md} and
\texttt{cancellation/BOUNDARY\_GEOMETRY.md}.

The canonical degreewise comparison theorem is
\texttt{papers/marked-root-multiplicity/main.tex}.  It proves that for every
$N\ge4$ there are at least
\[
 1+(N-1)\tau(N-1)-\sigma(N-1)
\]
pairwise stably inequivalent maps of generic degree $N$, including all
parameter-root branches of the cancellation construction and one weighted
class.  The separate positive-dimensional weighted-moduli theorem and its
full-cover faithfulness refinement are in
\texttt{extended-geometry/DECORATED\_NORMALIZATION\_INVARIANT.md}.

These subsequent results are not inputs to this paper.  The argument here
continues to depend only on the explicit cubic identities, the two projective
root charts, normalization and Zariski's Main Theorem, and purity for the
symmetric-monodromy statement.
