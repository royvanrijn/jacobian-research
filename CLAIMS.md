# Claim ledger

This ledger records the evidentiary status of each major theorem stated in
the README.  “Executable certificate” means an exact computation of the
finite identity or model named in that cell.  It does not silently promote a
bounded computation into a proof of an all-degree quantifier.  “Regression
only” lists computations whose role is testing a written uniform proof.

The verification target names refer to the targets in the repository
`Makefile`.  An unchecked independent audit means that the repository has not
yet recorded a clean-room proof or execution independent of the present
arguments and implementations.

## Known gaps exposed by the ledger

- The scripts accompanying C04--C11 verify identities and bounded examples;
  they are not formal proofs of the all-degree quantifiers.
- C13 still needs effective good-prime exclusions and a claim-by-claim audit
  of the finite-cover Chebotarev hypotheses.
- C15 relies on external reduction theorems whose hypotheses are explained but
  have not been independently re-audited in a second proof environment.
- No all-degree family theorem has a formal proof-assistant artifact.
- C16 classifies prime divisors on the normalized graph; an explicit minimal
  sequence of blow-ups and its discrepancy data have not yet been computed.
- C17 solves the isolated affine-difference block; the global equalizer which
  couples compensating transfers at several roots remains open.
- Several exact scripts under `scripts/` are exploratory or ancillary and are
  intentionally outside `make verify`; their absence is not evidence for a
  theorem unless a ledger row names them.
