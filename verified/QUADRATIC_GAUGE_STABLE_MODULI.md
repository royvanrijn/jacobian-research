# Stable moduli of the quadratic-gauge family

This note audits the proposed `(N-3)`-dimensional quadratic-gauge moduli
theorem.  The proposal overlooks an independent scaling of the first target
coordinate.  After including that scaling, the exact stable-moduli dimension
on the coefficient-torus clean locus is

\[
 \boxed{N-4,}
\]

not `N-3`.

The result is stronger than a dimension upper bound: on this locus it
classifies stable polynomial left--right equivalence exactly.

Work over an algebraically closed characteristic-zero field `k`, and fix
`N>=4`.  Divide the seed by `g_1` and use the target shear which removes
`g_2`.  Write

\[
 h_P(S)
 =S+a_3PS^3+\sum_{j=4}^Na_jP^jS^j,                    \tag{1}
\]

where every `a_j` is nonzero.  Denote this coefficient-torus locus by

\[
 \mathcal U_N=\operatorname{Spec}
 k[a_3^{\pm1},a_4^{\pm1},\ldots,a_N^{\pm1}]
 \simeq\mathbb G_m^{N-2}.                              \tag{2}
\]

The associated normalized inverse equation is

\[
 h_P(S)-\frac12(BS^2+C)=0.                              \tag{3}
\]

## Stable-moduli theorem

Let `a,b in U_N`.  Their quadratic-gauge maps are stably polynomially
left--right equivalent if and only if there are
`alpha,beta in k^times` such that

\[
 \boxed{
 b_3=\alpha^{-2}\beta^{-1}a_3,\qquad
 b_j=\alpha^{1-j}\beta^{-j}a_j\quad(4\le j\le N).
 }                                                       \tag{4}
\]

Consequently the coarse stable-moduli quotient on `U_N` is the torus quotient

\[
 \mathcal M_N^{\rm quad}
 =\mathcal U_N/\mathbb G_m^2
\]

and

\[
 \boxed{\dim\mathcal M_N^{\rm quad}=N-4.}               \tag{5}
\]

For `N=4` the coarse quotient is one point and the quotient stack has generic
stabilizer `mu_5`.  For `N>=5` the action is faithful and the quotient is a
torus of dimension `N-4`.

The theorem remains valid over an arbitrary characteristic-zero field after
interpreting the statement geometrically over its algebraic closure.

## 1. The overlooked second scaling

Let `F_a` denote the map associated with (1).  For
`alpha,beta in k^times`, put

\[
 \sigma_{\alpha,\beta}(x,y,z)
   =(\alpha x,\alpha^{-1}y,\beta z),                    \tag{6}
\]

\[
 \tau_{\alpha,\beta}(P,B,C)
   =(\beta P,\alpha^{-1}B,\alpha C).                    \tag{7}
\]

If `b` is defined by (4), then

\[
 \boxed{
 F_b\circ\sigma_{\alpha,\beta}
 =\tau_{\alpha,\beta}\circ F_a.
 }                                                       \tag{8}
\]

Indeed `t=1+xy` is fixed.  If

\[
 q_a=t^2z+a_3^{-1}y^2(1+3t),
\]

then (4) gives

\[
 q_b\bigl(\sigma_{\alpha,\beta}(x,y,z)\bigr)=\beta q_a.
\]

Substitution in the three coordinates of the denominator-free map gives
respectively the factors `beta`, `alpha^{-1}`, and `alpha` in (7).  Thus
every orbit in (4) consists of maps which are already polynomially
left--right equivalent, without stabilization.

The `alpha` action is the expected normalization-coordinate scaling.  The
`beta` action scales `P=tq`; it is independent because the cubic skeleton in
(1) has `P`-weight one while the terms of degree at least four have
`P`-weight equal to their degree.

If `a_2` is retained, it transforms as

\[
 b_2=\alpha^{-1}\beta^{-1}a_2.
\]

It supplies no modulus because

\[
 (P,B,C)\longmapsto(P,B-2a_2P,C)                       \tag{9}
\]

removes it by a polynomial target shear.  Multiplying the original seed by
a nonzero scalar likewise changes no normalized coefficient.  Hence (2)
is the full coefficient space after the two elementary redundancies have
been removed.

## 2. Intrinsic base character and the puncture pair

The
[quadratic-versus-weighted stable-separation theorem](QUADRATIC_WEIGHTED_STABLE_SEPARATION.md)
proves that the canonical Zariski--Main boundary has two intrinsically
ordered target images `(Z_Delta,Z_0)`, with `Z_0=V(P)` in the displayed
coordinates.  After deleting `Z_0`, the normalized ramified target stratum is

\[
 \widetilde Z_\Delta^\circ
 =\operatorname{Spec}k[P^{\pm1},r^{\pm1}]
 \simeq\mathbb G_m^2.                                  \tag{10}
\]

The character `P` is intrinsic up to a scalar.  Indeed `Z_0` is an
intrinsically selected reduced prime hypersurface in the affine target.
After any identity stabilization, an automorphism carrying this hypersurface
to its counterpart carries a prime generator to a unit times a prime
generator.  The only units of the ambient polynomial ring are `k^times`, so

\[
 P'\longmapsto\beta P.                                 \tag{11}
\]

Modulo the subgroup generated by `P`, the unit lattice of (10) has rank one.
Its primitive generator gives an unordered pair of relative toric punctures
`r=0,infinity`.  Hence an isomorphism of stabilized intrinsic strata has the
form

\[
 P'\longmapsto\beta P,\qquad
 r'\longmapsto\alpha P^m r^\varepsilon,
 \qquad \varepsilon\in\{1,-1\},                        \tag{12}
\]

for some `m in Z`.  Polynomial stabilization introduces no additional
units, so no stabilization variable can occur in (12).  The intrinsic
Fitting divisor below excludes `epsilon=-1`, thereby ordering the two
punctures without selecting either displayed target coordinate `B` or `C`.

## 3. The intrinsic Fitting divisor

Put

\[
 R_a=k[P^{\pm1},B(r),C(r)]
 \subset A=k[P^{\pm1},r^{\pm1}].
\]

In displayed coordinates,

\[
 \begin{aligned}
 B(r)&=r^{-1}+3a_3Pr+
       \sum_{j=4}^Nja_jP^jr^{j-2},\\
 C(r)&=r-a_3Pr^3+
       \sum_{j=4}^N(2-j)a_jP^jr^j.                     \tag{13}
 \end{aligned}
\]

The finite map `Spec(A)->Spec(R_a)` is the normalization of the intrinsic
ramified target stratum.  Since `P lies in R_a`, relative differentiation
gives

\[
 \Omega_{A/R_a}
 \simeq
 \frac{A\,dr}{(B_r\,dr,C_r\,dr)}.                       \tag{14}
\]

The envelope identity `C_r=-r^2B_r` and invertibility of `r` show that

\[
 \operatorname{Fitt}_0\Omega_{A/R_a}=(J_a),             \tag{15}
\]

where

\[
 \boxed{
 J_a(P,r)
 =r^2B_r
 =-1+3a_3Pr^2+
   \sum_{j=4}^Nj(j-2)a_jP^jr^{j-1}.
 }                                                       \tag{16}
\]

This is the quadratic-gauge analogue of the weighted Hessian divisor.  It
retains every coefficient `a_3,...,a_N` and is transported, with
multiplicity, by stable polynomial left--right equivalence through stable
normalization functoriality.

Suppose now that `F_a` and `F_b` are stably equivalent.  Equations
(11)--(12) and Fitting functoriality give

\[
 J_b(\beta P,\alpha P^m r^\varepsilon)
 =\lambda P^u r^vJ_a(P,r)                              \tag{17}
\]

for some `lambda in k^times` and `u,v in Z`.  Every coefficient is nonzero,
so the support of either Fitting polynomial is exactly

\[
 \{(0,0),(1,2),(4,3),(5,4),\ldots,(N,N-1)\}.            \tag{18}
\]

First suppose `epsilon=-1`.  Up to translation by the unit `r^v`, the
set of `r`-exponents on the left is

\[
 \{0,1,\ldots,N-3,N-1\},
\]

whose unique missing interior exponent is `N-2`.  The right-hand set is

\[
 \{0,2,3,\ldots,N-1\},
\]

whose unique missing interior exponent is one.  These sets differ for every
`N>=4`.  Thus `epsilon=1`: the Fitting divisor intrinsically orders the two
punctures.

Comparison of the smallest and largest `r`-exponents in (17) now gives `v=0`.
The unique term of `r`-degree zero then gives `u=0` and `lambda=1`.  The
unique term of `r`-degree two has `P`-degree `1+2m` on the left and degree
one on the right, so

\[
 m=0.                                                   \tag{19}
\]

Coefficient comparison in (17) now gives exactly (4).  Hence stable
equivalence implies membership in one of the explicit ordinary
left--right orbits from Section 1.  This proves both directions of the
classification theorem.

For reference, once normalized coordinates have been recovered, (13)
satisfies

\[
 h_P(r)=\frac12\bigl(C(r)+r^2B(r)\bigr).                \tag{20}
\]

In the unnormalized convention this is

\[
 G_P(r)=\frac{g_1}{2}\bigl(C(r)+r^2B(r)\bigr),
\]

not `(2/g_1)(C+r^2B)`.

## 4. Dimension and stabilizers

The coefficient torus (2) has dimension `N-2`.  The weights of the
`(alpha,beta)` action are

\[
 (-2,-1),\quad(1-j,-j)\quad(4\le j\le N).              \tag{21}
\]

The first two weights, for `a_3,a_4`, have determinant five, so the action
has rank two.  This proves (5).

For `N=4`, the action map `G_m^2->G_m^2` is an isogeny with kernel `mu_5`.
It is transitive on geometric points, giving a one-point coarse quotient.
For `N>=5`, the determinants of the pairs `(a_3,a_4)` and `(a_3,a_5)` are
five and six.  Their gcd is one, so the weights span the full character
lattice and the action is faithful.  The geometric quotient is therefore,
noncanonically,

\[
 \mathbb G_m^{N-4}.                                    \tag{22}
\]

## 5. Consequence for the two-family picture

The weighted clean locus has stable-moduli dimension `N-3`.  The quadratic
coefficient-torus locus has dimension `N-4`, and the stable-separation theorem
shows that no stable orbit crosses between them.  Thus the repository now
contains two stably disjoint positive-dimensional loci for every `N>=5`,
but not two `(N-3)`-dimensional loci from these two constructions.

As before, “distinct components” in the connected or irreducible sense
requires a constructed ambient moduli space and a proof about closures.
The theorem here is an exact stable-orbit classification on the stated clean
locus.
