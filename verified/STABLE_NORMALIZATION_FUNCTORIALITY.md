# Stable normalization functoriality

This note is the construction-independent source for every stabilization
argument involving canonical finite normalizations, their boundaries, and
scheme-theoretic decorations.  Later stable-boundary theorems should cite this
result instead of reproving polynomial-base-change statements locally.

Let `k` be a field, let `Y=Spec(A)` be an integral affine `k`-variety,
let `L/k(Y)` be a finite extension, and assume that the integral closure
`B` of `A` in `L` is finite over `A`.  (This finiteness is automatic for
the finite-type `k`-algebras used in this repository.)  Put

\[
 X=\operatorname{Norm}_Y(L)=\operatorname{Spec}(B).
\]

Choose a dense open `U` in `X` and write
`partial=(X\setminus U)_red`.  For `s>=0`, put

\[
 Y_s=Y\times\mathbb A^s,\qquad U_s=U\times\mathbb A^s,
 \qquad L_s=L(t_1,\ldots,t_s).
\]

## Theorem

There is a canonical isomorphism over `Y_s`

\[
 \boxed{\operatorname{Norm}_{Y\times\mathbb A^s}(L_s)
 \simeq X\times\mathbb A^s.}                         \tag{1}
\]

Under (1):

1. The reduced boundary is
   \[
   (X\times\mathbb A^s\setminus U_s)_{\rm red}
   =\partial\times\mathbb A^s,
   \]
   and its prime divisors are exactly `E x A^s`, for prime divisors `E`
   of `partial`.
2. If `E` lies over a target prime divisor `Z`, the stabilized valuation is
   the Gauss extension
   \[
   v_{E_s}|_L=v_E,\qquad v_{E_s}(t_i)=0,
   \]
   and
   \[
   e(E_s/Z_s)=e(E/Z),\qquad f(E_s/Z_s)=f(E/Z).       \tag{2}
   \]
3. Every reduced or scheme-theoretic multiple intersection commutes with
   stabilization.  Thus an affine intersection ring `R` becomes
   `R[t_1,...,t_s]`.
4. Nilradicals satisfy
   \[
   \operatorname{Nil}(R[t_1,\ldots,t_s])
   =\operatorname{Nil}(R)[t_1,\ldots,t_s],           \tag{3}
   \]
   so reducedness and the exact nilpotency index are preserved.
5. For every finitely presented module `M` on any affine stratum,
   \[
   \operatorname{Fitt}_i(M\otimes_RR[t_1,\ldots,t_s])
   =\operatorname{Fitt}_i(M)R[t_1,\ldots,t_s].       \tag{4}
   \]
   In particular, relative differentials and their Fitting divisors commute
   with stabilization:
   \[
   \Omega_{(X'\times\mathbb A^s)/(X\times\mathbb A^s)}
   \simeq \operatorname{pr}_1^*\Omega_{X'/X}.        \tag{5}
   \]
6. Fiber products, diagonal subschemes, saturated off-diagonal closures, and
   conductor ideals commute with this flat base change whenever they are
   formed from the finite normalization morphism.  Hence node-pairing and
   conductor decorations simply acquire an `A^s` factor.

Consequently any invariant functorially defined from a fixed pair `(X,U)`,
its labeled divisorial valuations, scheme intersections, relative
differentials, Fitting ideals, fiber products, or conductor is preserved by
adjoining identity variables.  The passage from this statement to stable
polynomial left--right equivalence requires the map-intrinsic construction
below; it does not follow from an arbitrary choice of `U`.

## Proof

The ring `B[t_1,...,t_s]` is finite and integral over
`A[t_1,...,t_s]`, has fraction field `L_s`, and is normal because a polynomial
ring over a normal domain is normal.  Conversely, an element of `L_s`
integral over `A[t_1,...,t_s]` is integral over `B[t_1,...,t_s]`, hence lies
in that normal ring.  This proves (1).

If `I` is the radical boundary ideal in `B`, then `IB[t]` is radical because
its quotient is `(B/I)[t]`, which is reduced.  Minimal primes extend to
minimal primes of the same height.  At height one, the old uniformizer is
still a uniformizer and the residue-field extension is

\[
 \kappa(E)(t_1,\ldots,t_s)/\kappa(Z)(t_1,\ldots,t_s).
\]

This proves the boundary assertion and (2).

Scheme intersection is defined by sums of ideals, and polynomial extension
is flat, so both sums and their quotients commute with base change.  Since
`(R/Nil(R))[t]` is reduced, every nilpotent polynomial has nilpotent
coefficients; conversely finitely many nilpotent coefficients generate a
nilpotent ideal.  This proves (3), including preservation of the least
nilpotency exponent.

Fitting ideals are generated by minors of a presentation matrix.  Tensoring
a finite presentation with the flat ring `R[t]` extends exactly those minors,
which proves (4).  The standard base-change isomorphism for Kähler
differentials gives (5).  Fiber products and diagonals commute with arbitrary
base change; ideal quotients defining the relevant saturated closures commute
here with the faithfully flat polynomial extension.  Finally the conductor
is the annihilator of the finite quotient `nu_*O_X/O_D`; flat base change
extends that annihilator.  These observations prove the last assertion.

## Use in the repository

The theorem is deliberately independent of the weighted and cancellation
constructions.  Family-specific stable-boundary results need only establish
that their boundary or decoration is intrinsic to the canonical finite
normalization.  All stabilization, Fitting/base-change, valuation, and
nilpotency claims then follow from this note.
