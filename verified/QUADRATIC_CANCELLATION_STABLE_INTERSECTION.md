# Stable intersection of the quadratic-gauge and cancellation families

This note classifies the stable polynomial left--right intersection of the
root-engineered quadratic-gauge family with the full `(m,r)` cancellation
family.  The answer is smaller than the shared terminology suggests:

\[
 \boxed{\text{the only common stable class is the foundational cubic.}}
 \tag{1}
\]

In particular, the higher rungs of the quadratic stationary-point ladder are
the `m=1` cancellation column, but they are not members of the
root-engineered quadratic-gauge family up to stable polynomial left--right
equivalence.

Work over a characteristic-zero field `k`.  Geometric boundary statements
may be checked after extension to an algebraic closure.  For the
quadratic-gauge family assume

\[
 G(S)=g_1S+\cdots+g_NS^N,\qquad g_1g_3g_N\ne0.
 \tag{2}
\]

No nonvanishing hypothesis is imposed on the intermediate coefficients.
For the cancellation family allow every normalized parameter root and every
polynomially removable tail.

## Stable-intersection theorem

Let `F_G^quad` have degree `N`, and let `F_{m,r,q}^can` be a cancellation
map associated with a root `q` of `M_{m,r}`.  Then

\[
 F_G^{\rm quad}\times\operatorname{id}_{\mathbb A^s}
 \quad\text{and}\quad
 F_{m,r,q}^{\rm can}\times\operatorname{id}_{\mathbb A^s}
 \tag{3}
\]

are polynomially left--right equivalent for some `s>=0` if and only if

\[
 N=3,\qquad (m,r,q)=(1,1,3).
 \tag{4}
\]

In that case both maps are already polynomially left--right equivalent,
without stabilization, to the foundational cubic.

For every `N>=4` the two families are stably disjoint.  This includes every
factorization

\[
 N-1=(m+1)r,
 \tag{5}
\]

every root of the corresponding cancellation parameter polynomial, and
every admissible quadratic-gauge seed, including seeds with vanishing
intermediate coefficients.

The proof has two independent final obstructions:

1. the Fitting divisor on the normalized ramified torus is binomial for
   cancellation and has two-dimensional Laurent support for a quadratic
   gauge;
2. the scheme-theoretic intersection of the two target boundary images has
   nilpotency index `mr(m+1)` for cancellation and exactly `2` for a
   quadratic gauge.

The second calculation is also an all-degree explanation of the bounded
degrees `4,5,6,7`.

## 1. Common coarse invariants and the first reduction

Stable polynomial left--right equivalence preserves the generic degree, so
(5) is necessary.  Both inverse equations have symmetric generic monodromy
when `r` is odd; for even `r` the cancellation monodromy is alternating and
already differs from the quadratic-gauge `S_N`.  Monodromy is not needed
below.

The complete boundary ledgers intrinsically select the discriminant vertex
in both families.  For a quadratic gauge, the generic inverse derivative has
`N-1` simple zeros.  Its ramified boundary prime over the reduced
discriminant has ramification index two.  For cancellation,

\[
 \partial_T\Psi=C\{1-T(Q-PT)^m\}^r,
 \tag{6}
\]

so its critical partition is `r^(m+1)` and its discriminant boundary prime
has index `r+1`.  Thus equality of the ramified profiles forces `r=1`.

The complete boundary diagrams give a slightly sharper bounded prefilter.
Put

\[
 d=N-3,\qquad h=\gcd(d,2).
 \tag{7}
\]

For a quadratic gauge, the second target boundary image has `h` boundary
primes, each of index `d/h`.  For cancellation it has `mr-1` boundary
primes, all unramified.  Hence the diagrams can agree only in degrees four
and five, for types `(2,1)` and `(3,1)`, respectively.  The stronger
invariants below exclude those two survivors and simultaneously exclude
every other noncubic type.

There is one important neutral invariant.  After deleting the second target
boundary image, both normalized ramified strata are two-dimensional tori.
For cancellation, put `Y=Q-PT` on the critical divisor.  Then

\[
 T=Y^{-m},\qquad P=(Q-Y)Y^m.
 \tag{8}
\]

After inverting `P`, set `u=(Q-Y)/Y`.  The normalization ring becomes

\[
 k[Y^{\pm1},u^{\pm1}].
 \tag{9}
\]

For a quadratic gauge the corresponding ring is
`k[P^{\pm1},S^{\pm1}]`.  Thus both unit lattices have rank two.  Raw unit
rank and puncture count do not classify the intersection.

## 2. Fitting-divisor obstruction on the ramified torus

The common torus makes its relative Fitting divisor decisive.

### Cancellation

Write

\[
 A_{\rm can}=k[Q,Y,Y^{-1}]
 \tag{10}
\]

for the normalization of the reduced cancellation discriminant, with `P`
as in (8), and let `R_can` be the image ring generated by the three target
coordinates.  Relative to `Q`,

\[
 P_Y=Y^{m-1}\{mQ-(m+1)Y\}.
 \tag{11}
\]

If `R(P,Q,T)` denotes the critical value before substituting (8), then

\[
 \frac{dR}{dY}=R_P P_Y+R_T T_Y=R_P P_Y
 \tag{12}
\]

because `R_T=C(1-T(Q-PT)^m)^r` vanishes on the critical divisor.  Therefore

\[
 \operatorname{Fitt}_0
 \Omega_{A_{\rm can}/R_{\rm can}}
 =\bigl(mQ-(m+1)Y\bigr).
 \tag{13}
\]

On the punctured torus (9), `Q=(u+1)Y`, so, up to the unit `Y`,

\[
 \boxed{J_{\rm can}=m u-1.}
 \tag{14}
\]

Its Laurent support has two points and affine rank one.  Neither `r` nor the
cancellation parameter root `q` occurs in (14).

### Quadratic gauge

Divide (2) by `g_1` and shear away `g_2`.  Write

\[
 H_P(S)=S+a_3PS^3+\sum_{j=4}^Na_jP^jS^j,
 \qquad a_3a_N\ne0.
 \tag{15}
\]

The normalized inverse equation is

\[
 H_P(S)-\frac12(BS^2+C)=0.
 \tag{16}
\]

On the normalized discriminant,

\[
 B(S)=\frac{H_P'(S)}S,\qquad
 C(S)=2H_P(S)-SH_P'(S).
 \tag{17}
\]

The envelope identity `C_S=-S^2B_S` gives

\[
 \operatorname{Fitt}_0
 \Omega_{A_{\rm quad}/R_{\rm quad}}
 =(J_{\rm quad}),
 \tag{18}
\]

where

\[
 \boxed{
 J_{\rm quad}
 =-1+3a_3PS^2+
   \sum_{j=4}^Nj(j-2)a_jP^jS^{j-1}.
 }
 \tag{19}
\]

Even when every intermediate coefficient vanishes, its support contains

\[
 (0,0),\qquad(1,2),\qquad(N,N-1).
 \tag{20}
\]

These three points have affine rank two, since

\[
 \det\begin{pmatrix}1&2\\N&N-1\end{pmatrix}=-(N+1)\ne0.
 \tag{21}
\]

Every automorphism of a torus acts on Laurent exponents by an affine
`GL_2(Z)` transformation.  After identity stabilization the units are still
constant Laurent monomials, so the same statement holds on
`\mathbb G_m^2\times\mathbb A^s`.  Such a transformation preserves both
support cardinality and affine rank.  It cannot carry the binomial (14) to
the full-rank polynomial (19).

The ramified target vertex is intrinsic in both complete boundary diagrams,
and relative Fitting ideals commute with stabilization.  Equations
(14)--(21) therefore prove stable disjointness for every `N>=4`.

This also describes the relevant puncture-preserving automorphism issue:
both open normalizations admit monomial torus automorphisms, but no member
of that group can turn a translated subtorus into the quadratic-gauge
Fitting divisor.  Special arithmetic parameter roots cannot enlarge the
group because they do not enter (14).

## 3. Universal quadratic-gauge boundary trace

There is a second, independent obstruction which sees the conductor/contact
structure at the intersection of the two target boundary images.

Double (16) and put

\[
 \widehat E_N(S)
 =2S-BS^2-C+2a_3PS^3+
   \sum_{j=4}^N2a_jP^jS^j.
 \tag{22}
\]

Let `Disc_S(\widehat E_N)` be its ordinary degree-`N` discriminant.  For
every `N>=4`,

\[
 v_P\!\left(\operatorname{Disc}_S(\widehat E_N)\right)
 =N^2-3N-2.
 \tag{23}
\]

More precisely, its first nonzero `P`-coefficient is

\[
 \boxed{
 \left.
 P^{-(N^2-3N-2)}
 \operatorname{Disc}_S(\widehat E_N)
 \right|_{P=0}
 =
 (-1)^{(N-1)(N-2)/2}
 2^{2N-4}(N-3)^{N-3}
 a_3^{N-2}a_N^{N-4}
 B^2(BC-1).
 }
 \tag{24}
\]

The convention `(N-3)^(N-3)=1` applies at `N=3`, although (23) is only
used for `N>=4`.

### Proof of the initial discriminant

The lower Newton polygon of (22) has vertices

\[
 (0,0),\quad(2,0),\quad(3,1),\quad(N,N).
 \tag{25}
\]

Every point `(j,j)`, `4<=j<N`, lies strictly above the last edge.  Put
`d=N-3` and `lambda=(N-1)/d`.  The roots split into:

- two finite roots of `-C+2S-BS^2`;
- one root with leading term `B(2a_3P)^{-1}`;
- `d` roots `P^{-lambda}w`, where
  \[
   w^d=-a_3/a_N.
  \tag{26}
  \]

All three residual equations are separable over the generic coefficient
field.  In

\[
 \operatorname{Disc}(f)
 =c_N^{2N-2}\prod_{i<j}(s_i-s_j)^2,
 \tag{27}
\]

their valuations give

\[
\begin{aligned}
 2N(N-1)
 &-4-4(N-1)-2(N-1)-(N-1)(N-4)\\
 &=N^2-3N-2,
\end{aligned}
 \tag{28}
\]

which proves (23).  The finite pair contributes

\[
 -\frac{4(BC-1)}{B^2}.
 \tag{29}
\]

The middle root contributes `B/(2a_3)`, and the far cluster uses

\[
 \operatorname{Disc}\left(
  W^d+\frac{a_3}{a_N}\right)
 =(-1)^{d(d-1)/2}d^d
  \left(\frac{a_3}{a_N}\right)^{d-1}.
 \tag{30}
\]

Combining (29)--(30) with the leading coefficient `2a_NP^N` gives exactly
(24).  Coefficients corresponding to points above the Newton polygon cannot
enter this initial term.  This proves the formula for every admissible seed,
not merely for the coefficient-torus locus.

The critical-value incidence is birational to the reduced discriminant and
is a generic simple fold, so the saturated ordinary discriminant is a
reduced prime equation.  Since `a_3a_N` is nonzero, the canonical
scheme-theoretic intersection of the two quadratic-gauge target boundary
images has coordinate ring

\[
\begin{aligned}
 I_{\rm quad}
 &=k[B,C]/\bigl(B^2(BC-1)\bigr)\\
 &\simeq k[B,C]/(B^2)\ \times\ k[B,B^{-1}].
\end{aligned}
 \tag{31}
\]

Its nilradical has exact nilpotency index two, and its reduction is
`\mathbb A^1\sqcup\mathbb G_m`.

For cancellation, the all-parameter thick-intersection theorem gives

\[
\begin{aligned}
 I_{\rm can}
 &=
 k[Q,R]\Big/
 \left(
 Q^{mr(m+1)}
 \bigl((r+1)RQ^m-C_0\bigr)
 \right)\\
 &\simeq
 k[Q,R]/\bigl(Q^{mr(m+1)}\bigr)
 \ \times\ k[Q,Q^{-1}],
\end{aligned}
 \tag{32}
\]

where `C_0` is the nonzero construction constant.  Its nilpotency index is

\[
 \mu_{\rm can}=mr(m+1).
 \tag{33}
\]

For every noncubic cancellation type, `mr>1`, hence
`\mu_can>=4`.  Polynomial stabilization tensors (31)--(32) with a
polynomial ring and preserves the nilradical index.  Thus (31)--(33) give a
second uniform stable obstruction.

The reductions of (31) and (32) are isomorphic.  Consequently reduced
incidence, component count, unit rank, and the coarse pairing of the
`\mathbb A^1` and `\mathbb G_m` pieces do not suffice.  The doubled
quadratic component versus the `mr(m+1)`-fold cancellation component is the
first conductor/contact layer which always separates them.  No further
node-pairing calculation is needed for the classification.

## 4. Degrees four through seven

The complete bounded ledger is:

| `N` | cancellation types `(m,r)` | `e_Delta=r+1` | cancellation `mu` | quadratic second-boundary primes | quadratic `mu` |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---:|
| 4 | `(2,1)` | `2` | `6` | `1` prime of index `1` | 2 |
| 5 | `(3,1)`, `(1,2)` | `2`, `3` | `12`, `4` | `2` primes of index `1` | 2 |
| 6 | `(4,1)` | `2` | `20` | `1` prime of index `3` | 2 |
| 7 | `(5,1)`, `(2,2)`, `(1,3)` | `2`, `3`, `4` | `30`, `12`, `6` | `2` primes of index `2` | 2 |

Only `(2,1)` in degree four and `(3,1)` in degree five survive the
ramification and boundary-prime prefilters.  Their thick indices `6` and
`12` differ from `2`.  Every parameter root within a row has the same
inverse resolvent, Fitting divisor (14), and thick index, so arithmetic
specialization creates no exceptional intersection.

The corresponding parameter polynomials are

\[
\begin{array}{c|l}
(m,r)&M_{m,r}(q)\\ \hline
(2,1)&q^2-4q+6\\
(3,1)&q^3-5q^2+10q-10\\
(1,2)&q^2-5q+10\\
(4,1)&q^4-6q^3+15q^2-20q+15\\
(5,1)&q^5-7q^4+21q^3-35q^2+35q-21\\
(2,2)&q^4-7q^3+21q^2-35q+35\\
(1,3)&q^3-7q^2+21q-35.
\end{array}
\tag{34}
\]

They are squarefree in characteristic zero, but neither their splitting
fields nor special Galois behavior affects the geometric obstruction.

## 5. The cubic equality and the ladder

Degree three forces `(m,r)=(1,1)`.  Its parameter polynomial is `q-3`, and
the normalized cancellation jet is `h(A)=3+9A`.  The cancellation map is a
linear reparametrization of the foundational cubic.

For a degree-three quadratic gauge, shear away `g_2`, divide by `g_1`, and
write `H_P(S)=S+a_3PS^3`.  The explicit source--target scaling of the
quadratic-gauge family sends any `a_3 in k^*` to `1`.  The resulting map is
exactly the foundational map with seed `S^3+S`.  This proves the equality
in (4) over the original ground field.

The stationary-point ladder has

\[
 (m,r)=(1,\ell-1),\qquad N=2\ell-1.
 \tag{35}
\]

Its first rung `ell=2` is the common cubic.  For every `ell>=3`, (14)--(21)
or (31)--(33) separates it from every degree-`2ell-1` root-engineered
quadratic gauge.  Thus “the quadratic ladder is the `m=1` cancellation
column” is compatible with (1): the name describes the quadratic critical
polynomial `1-BS+AS^2`, not an all-degree intersection with the
root-engineered quadratic-gauge family.
