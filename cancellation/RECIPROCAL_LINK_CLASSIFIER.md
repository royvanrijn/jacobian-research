# Exact reciprocal-link classifier

The classifier in
[`../jcsearch/reciprocal.py`](../jcsearch/reciprocal.py) turns the marked
reciprocal-link theorem into an exact candidate test.  It uses polynomial
division, Jacobian determinants, Groebner elimination, and gcds over rational
function fields.  No floating-point root approximations are used.

## Certificate pipeline

For a candidate `(A,s,P,Q,m,r)` in a polynomial source ring, the classifier
computes

\[
 Y=Q-sP,\qquad x=As,\qquad y=Y,\qquad B=P/A.
\]

It then certifies:

1. the five prime valuations `v_A(s),v_A(Y),v_A(P),v_A(B),v_A(D)`;
2. `D=1-sY^m=A^(-1)` and `A=1+xy^m`;
3. polynomiality of `(x,y,B)` and both localized coordinate compositions;
4. the chart and straightening Jacobians;
5. the coefficients, content, and value on `B` of
   `partial=Jac(x,y,-)`;
6. the boundary elimination ideal of `(A,X-x,Y-y,Z-B)`;
7. the minimal polynomial of `bar(B)` over a supplied rational boundary
   normalization chart and hence its displayed residue degree;
8. the **full** relative constant field of the generic boundary fiber,
   independently of `bar(B)`, and hence its automatic Stein degree;
9. whether `k(tau)(bar(B))` is the full Stein field or a proper visible
   subfield, in which case a hidden cover is reported;
10. the gcd of the displayed residue polynomial with the transformed spectral
   constraint

\[
 y^{(m+1)mr}J_{mr,r}(Z/y^{m+1}).
\]

A gcd of degree zero is an exact arithmetic certificate that the displayed
residue cannot occur in a polynomial cancellation core.  The full-Stein stage
does not trust a primitive declaration.  It divides the Jacobian derivation
by its polynomial content, reduces it to the generic boundary fiber over
`QQ(tau)`, constructs a rational local slice, and applies the finite
exponential invariantization map

\[
 \rho(a)=\sum_{i\geq0}{(-t)^i\over i!}\partial^i(a).
\]

The invariantized source coordinates generate the relative constant field.
Groebner elimination gives their finite relation algebra, whose vector-space
dimension is the full Stein degree.  A deterministic primitive-element search
also records one defining polynomial for that field.

Over the algebraically closed ground field, every transformed spectral factor
is linear; hence the classifier rejects every **displayed primitive** residue
polynomial of degree greater than one, even if an arithmetic gcd bundles
several conjugate constant roots.  A degree-one common factor identifies the
permitted boundary value `bar(B)=q y^(m+1)`.  A separate
`hidden_stein_cover_detected` flag records the logically different case in
which this displayed residue has degree one but the full Stein degree is
greater.

The final `verdict` is deliberately a prefilter verdict.  Passing means that
the exact reciprocal markings and boundary spectrum agree with the marked
cancellation theorem; it does not replace the separate check that the full
primitive `T` is polynomial.

## Command-line examples

```bash
.venv/bin/python scripts/classify_reciprocal_link.py cancellation
.venv/bin/python scripts/classify_reciprocal_link.py masuda
.venv/bin/python scripts/classify_reciprocal_link.py masuda-hidden
```

The first example returns valuations `(-1,0,1,0,-1)`, chart ratio `-1/A`,
LND value `partial(B)=A^2`, residue polynomial `Z-3*tau^2`, and Stein degree
one.  The second eliminates the standard plinth model to

\[
 Z^2-\tau,
\]

so its Stein degree is two.  The third deliberately displays only the
degree-one residue `Z=tau` on the same model.  The old residue-only test would
miss the cover; the automatic stage still returns primitive polynomial
`Z_Stein^2-tau`, full degree two, and `hidden_stein_cover_detected=true`.

## Supplying a new reciprocal candidate

```python
import sympy as sp

from jcsearch.reciprocal import (
    ReciprocalLinkCandidate,
    classify_reciprocal_link,
)

u, v, w = sp.symbols("u v w")
X, Y, Z = sp.symbols("X Y Z")
tau = sp.symbols("tau", nonzero=True)

A = 1 + u*v
B = A**2*w + 3*v**2

candidate = ReciprocalLinkCandidate(
    name="example",
    source_variables=(u, v, w),
    boundary=A,
    s=u/A,
    P=A*B,
    Q=v+u*B,
    m=1,
    r=1,
    coordinate_symbols=(X, Y, Z),
    inverse_source=(X, Y, (Z-3*Y**2)/(1+X*Y)**2),
    boundary_parameter=tau,
    boundary_parametrization={X: -1/tau, Y: tau},
    reconstruction_is_primitive=True,
)

certificate = classify_reciprocal_link(candidate)
```

`coordinate_symbols` must be independent symbols for `(x,y,B)`.  The inverse
formulas may use their boundary equation in the denominator.  The boundary
parametrization is a rational normalization chart for the base curve.  The
classifier computes both the degree generated by `bar(B)` and the full Stein
degree.  `reconstruction_is_primitive` is now only an expected marking;
`primitive_marking_agrees` checks it against the automatic result.

## General split-plinth obstruction

The obstruction does not depend on a special quadratic model.  If `H(Z)` is
irreducible of degree `f>1` over the boundary field and `bar(B)` is its root,
regularity of the Keller primitive forces

\[
 J_{mr,r}(\bar B/y^{m+1})=0.
\]

Over the algebraically closed ground field, every root of `J_(mr,r)` is a
constant.  Therefore `bar(B)=q y^(m+1)` belongs to the base field, contradicting
`deg H>1`.  The verifier checks the corresponding exact gcd for the family
`H(Z)=Z^f-tau`, `2<=f<=6`, and `1<=m,r<=4`; Proposition 8.6 in
[`LOG_GEOMETRY_OF_SUSPENSIONS.md`](LOG_GEOMETRY_OF_SUSPENSIONS.md) proves it
for every primitive displayed finite boundary extension.

## No-hidden-cover log lemma

The spectral equation collapses `bar(B)` to the base, but this alone does not
exclude a larger Stein field.  The remaining argument is log-valuative.  If
`L/k(y)` is the full finite Stein extension and `C_L` its normalization over
`G_m`, then

\[
 [L:k(y)]=\sum_{p\mid0}v_p(y)=-\sum_{p\mid\infty}v_p(y).
\]

Consequently a cover of degree `f>1` either has more than two punctures over
`{0,infinity}`, or—if there is one point over each end—the pulled-back unit
has vector `(f,-f)`, which is not primitive.  Puncture-complete
reconstruction data with vector `(1,-1)` therefore forces full Stein degree
one.  This is Lemma 8.6a in
[`LOG_GEOMETRY_OF_SUSPENSIONS.md`](LOG_GEOMETRY_OF_SUSPENSIONS.md).

## Scope

The current elimination backend expects rational coefficients, a rational
one-parameter normalization chart of the base boundary, an integral generic
fiber, and a nonzero induced primitive Jacobian LND.  It computes the full
Stein field automatically under those hypotheses; it does not infer from an
unmarked global compactification that every puncture has been recorded.
Groebner elimination can become expensive for high-degree source formulas;
the valuation, Jacobian, local-slice, and spectral stages remain inexpensive.
