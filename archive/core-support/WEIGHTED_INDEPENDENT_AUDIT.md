# Independent audit of the universal weighted marked-root theorem

This note rederives weighted marked-root theorem without importing the weighted-model implementation
or using its purity-based monodromy proof.  The accompanying checker uses only
Python's standard library and a clean-room sparse-polynomial implementation.

## Statement audited

Let `k` have characteristic zero and let `H in k[W]` have degree `n>=3` with

\[
H(0)=H(1)=H'(0)=0,\qquad H'(1)=-c\ne0,
\qquad \kappa=H''(1)/c\ne-2.
\]

For `b_0!=0`, set

\[
a_0=-{1+\kappa\over2+\kappa},\quad
v=xy,\quad S=x^2z,\quad u=1+v,
\quad\gamma=1+a_0v+b_0S,\quad W=u\gamma.
\]

With `p=H'` and `q=(Wp-H)/c`, define

\[
C=x\gamma,\qquad
B={c+p(W)/\gamma\over x},\qquad
A={u+q(W)/\gamma^2\over x^2}.                     \tag{1}
\]

The audit establishes independently that (1) is polynomial,
`det DG_H=b_0c`, and the source is the regular-reconstruction open in the
normalization of

\[
H(W)-BCW+cAC^2=0.                                  \tag{2}
\]

It also gives a second proof that `H(W)-sW+t` has geometric and arithmetic
monodromy `S_n`.

## 1. Polynomiality and determinant

Because `H'(0)=0`, one has `W|p`; because `H(0)=H'(0)=0`, one has `W^2|q`.
After `W=u gamma`, the displayed divisions by `gamma` are therefore
polynomial.  At `x=0`, `(u,gamma,W)=(1,1,1)`.  The numerator of `B` vanishes
there because `p(1)=-c`.  For the numerator of `A`,

\[
q(1)=-1,\qquad q'(1)=\kappa,
\]

so its value and first normal derivative vanish by

\[
1+\kappa+a_0(2+\kappa)=0.
\]

Thus the remaining numerators are divisible by `x` and `x^2` respectively.

For the determinant, introduce

\[
s=BC=c\gamma+p(W),\qquad
t=cAC^2=cW\gamma+Wp(W)-H(W).
\]

Direct differentiation gives the four independent determinants

\[
{\partial(x,v,S)\over\partial(x,y,z)}=x^3,
\quad
{\partial(W,\gamma,C)\over\partial(x,v,S)}=b_0\gamma^2,
\]

\[
{\partial(s,t,C)\over\partial(W,\gamma,C)}=-c^2\gamma,
\quad
{\partial(s,t,C)\over\partial(A,B,C)}=-cC^3.
\]

Since `C=x gamma`, the chain rule gives `det DG_H=b_0c` on a dense open and
hence everywhere.

The independent executable does not assume these determinants.  It expands
(1) with rational sparse-polynomial arithmetic, performs the exact monomial
divisions, differentiates the resulting polynomials, and recomputes the full
Jacobian determinant.

## 2. Incidence and reconstruction

Equations (1) imply

\[
BC=c\gamma+p(W),\qquad cAC^2=cW\gamma+Wp(W)-H(W).
\]

Using `W=u gamma`, subtraction proves (2).  On the incidence, differentiation
in `W` gives

\[
E_W=H'(W)-BC=-c\gamma.                              \tag{3}
\]

On `C!=0`, a simple marked root has `gamma!=0` by (3), and the inverse is

\[
\gamma={BC-H'(W)\over c},\quad x={C\over\gamma},
\quad u={W\over\gamma},\quad y={u-1\over x},
\]

\[
z={\gamma-1-a_0(u-1)\over b_0x^2}.                 \tag{4}
\]

Substitution into (1) proves both compositions.  The clean-room checker
verifies the denominator-free forms of all these identities independently.

The incidence is integral: as a polynomial linear in `A`, its coefficients
`cC^2` and `H(W)-BCW` are coprime.  It is finite flat of degree `n` over the
target because its equation is monic in `W` after constant rescaling.  The
source marking is birational by (4), and normality of affine three-space
factors it through the incidence normalization.  Its nonzero Jacobian makes
the lift quasi-finite, so Zariski's Main Theorem makes it an open immersion.
Wherever the three functions (4) are regular, the same fraction-field
identities provide its inverse.  Its image is therefore exactly the
simultaneous regular-reconstruction open.  This also shows directly why the
simple-root description is valid over `C!=0` but need not be valid at `C=0`.

## 3. Independent vertical-line proof of `S_n`

First, `H(W)-sW+t` is geometrically irreducible.  In any factorization over
an algebraic closure, one factor has `t`-degree zero; comparison of the unit
coefficient of `t` makes that factor a unit.  Gauss's lemma passes to
`k(s,t)[W]`.

A repeated root `r` gives

\[
(s,t)=(H'(r),rH'(r)-H(r)).                          \tag{5}
\]

The two coordinate functions in (5) have coprime pole orders `n-1` and `n`,
so (5) is generically one-to-one.  Hence the discriminant is irreducible and
its generic point represents exactly one double root.

Now work over `C` and choose a generic vertical line `s=s_0`.  Birationality
of (5) implies that this line meets the discriminant in `n-1` distinct smooth
points.  Equivalently, the polynomial map

\[
W\longmapsto -H(W)+s_0W
\]

has `n-1` simple critical points with distinct critical values.  Each finite
branch loop acts on the roots by a transposition.  The cover of the punctured
`t`-line is connected because `H(W)-s_0W+t` is irreducible, and its monodromy
is generated by those finite branch loops.  A transitive permutation group
generated by transpositions is `S_n`: form the graph whose edges are the
generating transpositions; transitivity makes the graph connected, and edge
transpositions of a connected graph generate the full symmetric group.

Thus the geometric monodromy is `S_n`.  The arithmetic group contains it and
is a subgroup of `S_n`, so it is also `S_n`.  Descent to a finitely generated
coefficient field and an embedding into `C` gives the same conclusion over
every characteristic-zero base field.  This proof uses one-variable branch
cycles rather than the purity and affine-plane fundamental-group argument in
the original proof.

## Independent executable

Run

```bash
python3 scripts/audit_weighted_independent.py
```

The script imports neither SymPy nor any project module.  Its custom exact
arithmetic checks:

- polynomiality and the full three-variable Jacobian in canonical inverse
  degrees four through ten;
- the marked-incidence and derivative identities;
- denominator-free reconstruction identities; and
- three noncanonical admissible primitives.

The all-degree quantifiers are supplied by the coefficient-independent proofs
above; the finite calculations are independent regression witnesses, not the
basis for extrapolation.
