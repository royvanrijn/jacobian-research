# C24 scheme-theoretic boundary intersections for all parameters

This note computes the full intersection of the discriminant divisor and
`P=0` for every `C24_(m,r)`.  For every noncubic pair
`(m,r)!=(1,1)`, both are intrinsic boundary-image divisors, so this is a
canonical stable boundary intersection.  Its reduced support is
`A^1 disjoint-union G_m`, while the affine-line component carries a
nilpotent thickening of index `mr(m+1)`.  In the cubic case, the same algebraic
trace formula holds but `P=0` is not a boundary-image divisor.

Let

\[
 \Psi(T)=C\int_0^T\{1-t(Q-Pt)^m\}^r dt-R,
 \qquad C\in k^*,\quad m,r\geq1.                              \tag{1}
\]

The critical divisor is

\[
 1-T(Q-PT)^m=0.                                               \tag{2}
\]

Put `Y=Q-PT`.  Its normalization is parametrized by

\[
 T=Y^{-m},\qquad P=(Q-Y)Y^m.                                 \tag{3}
\]

It is integral and maps generically one-to-one to the target discriminant.
The target discriminant is therefore a reduced prime divisor; the higher
power `r` in `Psi_T` controls ramification but does not thicken the prime
divisor itself.

## 1. Polynomial critical-value elimination

Define

\[
 F(Y)=P-(Q-Y)Y^m                                           \tag{4}
\]

and

\[
 G(Y)=C I_{m,r}(Y,Q)-RY^{m(r+1)},                           \tag{5}
\]

where

\[
 I_{m,r}(Y,Q)=
 \int_0^1\left[Y^m-u\{Q+(Y-Q)u\}^m\right]^rdu.              \tag{6}
\]

Indeed, substituting `T=Y^(-m)` and then `t=uY^(-m)` in (1)
shows that the critical-value equation becomes `G(Y)=0` after multiplication
by `Y^(m(r+1))`.  Both (4) and (5) are polynomials.

The equation `F` is monic in `Y`.  Since the critical-value incidence is
integral and generically birational to the discriminant, the primitive
resultant

\[
 \operatorname{Res}_Y(F,G)                                  \tag{7}
\]

is, up to a nonzero scalar and harmless coefficient-field units, the reduced
prime equation of the discriminant.  There is no generator multiplicity in
(7).

## 2. Exact trace on `P=0`

At `P=0`, equation (4) becomes

\[
 F(Y)=Y^m(Y-Q).                                             \tag{8}
\]

The two required values of (6) are beta integrals:

\[
 I_{m,r}(0,Q)
 =(-1)^rQ^{mr}\int_0^1u^r(1-u)^{mr}du
 =(-1)^rQ^{mr}{r!(mr)!\over(mr+r+1)!},                     \tag{9}
\]

and

\[
 I_{m,r}(Q,Q)=Q^{mr}\int_0^1(1-u)^rdu
 ={Q^{mr}\over r+1}.                                      \tag{10}
\]

Because (8) is monic,

\[
 \left.\operatorname{Res}_Y(F,G)\right|_{P=0}
 =G(0)^mG(Q).                                               \tag{11}
\]

Equations (9)--(11) give, up to a nonzero scalar and a power of the fixed
unit `C`,

\[
 \boxed{\quad
 Q^{mr(m+1)}\{(r+1)RQ^m-C\}.
 \quad}                                                     \tag{12}
\]

### Theorem 2.1 (all-parameter trace and noncubic thick intersection)

For every `m,r>=1`, the scheme-theoretic trace of the reduced discriminant
divisor on `P=0` has coordinate ring

\[
 \begin{aligned}
 \mathcal O(J_{\Delta,P})
 &=k[Q,R]/\left(Q^{mr(m+1)}\{(r+1)RQ^m-C\}\right)\\
 &\simeq k[Q,R]/(Q^{mr(m+1)})\times k[Q,Q^{-1}].             \tag{13}
 \end{aligned}
\]

Its nilradical has nilpotency index exactly

\[
 \boxed{mr(m+1)}.                                           \tag{14}
\]

Its reduction is

\[
 \mathcal O(I_{\Delta,P})
 \simeq k[R]\times k[Q,Q^{-1}],                             \tag{15}
\]

so the reduced intersection is `A^1 disjoint-union G_m` for every pair.

If `(m,r)!=(1,1)`, Theorem 5.1 of the exhaustion certificate shows that
`P=0` receives positive boundary contribution `mr-1` and that no other
boundary-image divisor exists, so both divisors are vertices of the canonical
boundary-incidence diagram.  In precisely these noncubic cases, (13) is a
canonical stable boundary intersection and (14) is a stable left--right
invariant.  For `(m,r)=(1,1)`, the `P=0` boundary contribution is zero; (13)
is then only a coordinate trace and carries no intrinsic marking claim.

**Proof.**  Formula (12) gives the scheme-theoretic trace of the reduced
prime discriminant on `P=0`.  Its two factors are comaximal because `C!=0`,
so the Chinese remainder theorem gives (13).  The first factor has
nilradical generated by `Q`, of exact index `mr(m+1)`; the second is reduced.
Taking radicals gives (15).  QED

For the noncubic pairs, polynomial stabilization tensors (13) with a
polynomial ring.  It therefore preserves the product decomposition, reduced
support, and exact nilpotency index by the canonical boundary-incidence
theorem.

For `r=1`, Theorem 2.1 specializes to the thick intersection used in
[the C04 comparison](BOUNDARY_INTERSECTION_OBSTRUCTION.md).  For `r>=2`, it
adds scheme structure to the already decisive ramification obstruction.

### Corollary 2.2 (uniform C04--C24 separation)

Let `(m,r)!=(1,1)` and put `N=r(m+1)+1`.  No `C24_(m,r)` map is polynomially
left--right equivalent, even stably, to a generic weighted C04 map of inverse
degree `N`.

**Proof.**  The
[boundary-exhaustion theorem](BOUNDARY_EXHAUSTION_CERTIFICATE.md), Theorem
5.1, shows that both maps have exactly two divisorial target boundary images.
For C24 their
scheme-theoretic intersection is nonreduced by (13)--(14).  For a generic
weighted seed, the exact saturated trace is

\[
 C=0,\qquad B^2-4h_2cA=0,
\]

whose coordinate ring is `k[B]` and is reduced.  The full boundary-incidence
diagram and its scheme-theoretic intersections are stable left--right
invariants, so the two diagrams cannot be isomorphic.  QED

For `(m,r)=(1,1)`, the second C24 boundary component has zero contribution and
the map is the known cubic C01 member, so the corollary correctly excludes
that case; Theorem 2.1 makes no intrinsic claim about its `P=0` trace.
