# Target-fixed rigidity of cancellation parameter branches

This note proves the first uniform equivalence theorem among distinct cancellation construction
parameter branches. It does not settle unrestricted polynomial left--right
equivalence, because a nontrivial target automorphism is still allowed there.
The general deck-transformation mechanism used below is isolated in
[the monodromy deck-rigidity theorem](MONODROMY_DECK_RIGIDITY.md).

Fix `m,r>=1`, a characteristic-zero field containing the relevant parameter
roots, and a nonzero Jacobian constant `C`. For a cancellation polynomial
`h`, write

\[
 A=1+xy^m,\qquad
 B_h=A^{r+1}z+y^{m+1}h(A),\qquad
 P_h=AB_h,\qquad Q_h=y+xB_h,
\]

and let `F_h=(P_h,Q_h,R_h)` be the cancellation map. Only the class of `h` modulo
`A^(r+1)` enters the cancellation condition.

## 1. The canonical cover is independent of the parameter

Put `s=x/A`. For every cancellation branch the target field extension is
generated by a root of

\[
 \Psi_{P,Q,R}(T)=
 C\int_0^T\{1-t(Q-Pt)^m\}^r\,dt-R.                    \tag{1}
\]

Equation (1), its canonical finite normalization, and its geometric
monodromy depend on `(m,r,C)` but not on `h` or its constant parameter `q`.
The geometric monodromy is `S_N` for odd `r` and `A_N` for even `r`, where

\[
 N=r(m+1)+1.
\]

The corresponding degree-`N` function-field extension has no nontrivial
target-field automorphism.  This is Corollary 1.2 of the general deck-rigidity
theorem: the natural point stabilizer `H` is self-normalizing in `S_N` for
`N>=3` and in `A_N` for `N>=4`.  Thus

\[
 \operatorname{Aut}_{k(P,Q,R)} k(x,y,z)=N_G(H)/H=1.    \tag{2}
\]

The even-`r` case always has `N>=5`, so these two cases exhaust cancellation construction.

## 2. The unique birational identification

Let `h` and `h'` be two cancellation polynomials.  Corollary 2.2 of the
general theorem says that target-fixed equivalence is equality of their two
marked affine opens inside the common normalization.  Any fixed-target
birational map must induce the unique field identification (2), hence fix
`s,P,Q`. The reconstruction formulas then fix

\[
 y=Q-sP,\qquad A=\{1-s(Q-Ps)^m\}^{-1},\qquad x=sA.
\]

Equality of `B_h` and `B_(h')` forces the last coordinate change

\[
 z'=z+{y^{m+1}\{h(A)-h'(A)\}\over A^{r+1}}.           \tag{3}
\]

Conversely, (3) fixes `B`, hence `P,Q,s`, and therefore also the integral
(1). It is the unique fixed-target birational equivalence.

Because `A=1+xy^m` is prime and coprime to `y`, formula (3) is polynomial if
and only if

\[
 h(A)\equiv h'(A)\pmod {A^{r+1}}.                     \tag{4}
\]

When (4) holds, (3) is the already-known polynomial source shift removing an
allowed tail, and its inverse is obtained by changing the sign.

### Theorem

Two cancellation maps with the same `(m,r,C)` are polynomially right-equivalent over
the identity target if and only if their cancellation polynomials agree
modulo `A^(r+1)`. In particular, normalized branches attached to distinct
roots `q!=q'` of `M_(m,r)` are not target-fixed right-equivalent.

The same statement holds after adjoining the same identity coordinates and
requiring the target, including those coordinates, to remain fixed. Purely
transcendental base change preserves the monodromy group and the
self-normalizing point stabilizer, so (2)--(4) are unchanged.

## 3. Boundary interpretation and the remaining problem

On the projective resolvent chart over `P=0`, put

\[
 K_{m,r}(w)=w^{-(r+1)}\int_0^w v^r(1-v)^{mr}\,dv.
\]

The parameter identity gives

\[
 K_{m,r}\left(-{q\over1-q}\right)=0.                  \tag{5}
\]

For the branch selected by `q`, this root is filled by the affine source
divisor `A=0`; the other roots represent missing boundary primes. Thus `q`
does not change the generic cover—it marks which projective `P=0` branch is
filled by affine three-space. The pole in (3) is exactly the failure to move
that marking by a target-fixed polynomial source automorphism.

Consequently any unrestricted left--right equivalence between distinct
parameter branches must use a nonidentity target automorphism whose lift to
the canonical finite normalization carries the filled branch in (5) to the
other filled branch and preserves the affine-source open. The remaining
problem is therefore reduced to the automorphism action on this canonical
normal pair; generic resolvent, monodromy, and numerical ramification data
alone cannot decide it.

The first computation of this group is now in
[TARGET_BOUNDARY_AUTOMORPHISM_GROUP.md](TARGET_BOUNDARY_AUTOMORPHISM_GROUP.md):
every labelled-pair automorphism restricts on `P=0` to
`(Q,R)->(uQ,u^(-m)R)`.  The resulting scaling torus fixes the projective
coordinate `w=PT/Q` and hence every parameter root.  Any motion between
distinct roots must therefore lie in the residual congruence kernel which is
identity on `P=0` and lifts to the finite normalization.
