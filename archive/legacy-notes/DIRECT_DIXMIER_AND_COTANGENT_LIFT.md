# Direct Dixmier consequence and cotangent lift

This note records two direct consequences of the displayed three-dimensional
Keller counterexample.  They do **not** use the large stable-equivalence models
of C15.

The construction below was circulated on 21 July 2026 in
[`wmayner/dixmier-counterexample`](https://github.com/wmayner/dixmier-counterexample),
where a self-contained note and an exact-arithmetic audit are provided.  The
present repository records the argument and its relation to the existing claim
structure; a clean-room local reimplementation is still pending.

Let

\[
F=(F_1,F_2,F_3):\mathbb A^3\longrightarrow\mathbb A^3
\]

be the displayed map, with

\[
\det DF=-2.
\]

Write `R=k[x_1,x_2,x_3]` over a characteristic-zero field `k`.

## 1. Polynomial inverse-Jacobian derivations

Since `det DF` is a unit, the inverse matrix

\[
G=(DF)^{-1}={\operatorname{adj}(DF)\over -2}
\]

has polynomial entries.  Define derivations

\[
D_i=\sum_{j=1}^3 G_{ij}\,{\partial\over\partial x_j}.
\]

Then

\[
D_i(F_j)=\delta_{ij}.
\]

The derivations commute.  Indeed, for every `j`,

\[
[D_i,D_l](F_j)=0.
\]

Because `k(F_1,F_2,F_3)\subset k(x_1,x_2,x_3)` is a finite separable function-
field extension, a derivation of `k(x_1,x_2,x_3)` vanishing on all `F_j` is zero.
Hence

\[
[D_i,D_l]=0.
\]

## 2. The Weyl-algebra endomorphism

Let `A_3(k)` be the third Weyl algebra, generated by `X_i,\partial_i` with

\[
[X_i,X_j]=[\partial_i,\partial_j]=0,
\qquad [\partial_i,X_j]=\delta_{ij}.
\]

The assignments

\[
\Psi(X_i)=F_i,
\qquad
\Psi(\partial_i)=D_i
\]

preserve these relations and therefore define an endomorphism

\[
\Psi:A_3(k)\longrightarrow A_3(k).
\]

Using the order filtration and principal symbols, or the usual simplicity
argument for Weyl algebras, `\Psi` is injective.

The external note proves the exact image description

\[
\operatorname{im}\Psi
 =\bigoplus_{\alpha\in\mathbb N^3} k[F_1,F_2,F_3]D^\alpha.
\]

In particular, `\Psi` is surjective exactly when

\[
k[F_1,F_2,F_3]=k[x_1,x_2,x_3],
\]

which would make `F` a polynomial automorphism.  Since `F` has a three-point
fiber, `\Psi` is not surjective.

There is also a direct explicit obstruction: multiplication by `x_1` is not in
the image.  If `x_1` belonged to `k[F]`, it would be constant on every fiber of
`F`, but on the displayed collision fiber it takes the three values `0,1,-1`.

Thus the counterexample gives an injective, non-surjective endomorphism of
`A_3(k)`.  Adjoining identity Weyl generators gives the same conclusion for
`A_n(k)` for every `n>=3`.

## 3. Cotangent lift

Let `q=(x_1,x_2,x_3)` and let `p` be cotangent coordinates.  Define

\[
\widehat F(q,p)=\bigl(F(q),\,DF(q)^{-T}p\bigr).
\]

Because `DF^{-1}` has polynomial entries, `\widehat F` is polynomial.  It is the
cotangent lift of the local etale map `F`, hence preserves the canonical
symplectic form

\[
\omega=\sum_i dp_i\wedge dq_i.
\]

It is noninjective: if `F(q)=F(q')`, then

\[
\widehat F(q,0)=\widehat F(q',0).
\]

The terminology should be kept precise: this is a polynomial map preserving the
symplectic form, but it is not a symplectomorphism because it has no inverse.

## 4. Relation to the repository

This route is dimension preserving and logically independent of C15:

\[
\text{three-dimensional Keller collision}
\Longrightarrow
\text{non-surjective endomorphism of }A_3
\Longrightarrow
\text{non-surjective endomorphisms of }A_n\ (n\ge3).
\]

It also gives a direct Poisson/symplectic lift in dimension six.

The proof depends only on:

1. the exact unit Jacobian determinant from C01;
2. noninjectivity from C01;
3. standard Weyl-algebra and derivation facts;
4. the image decomposition above.

## 5. Audit obligations before promotion to the claim ledger

Before assigning a new numbered claim, add a repository-local clean-room audit
which independently checks:

- the entries of `G=(DF)^{-1}`;
- `D_i(F_j)=delta_ij`;
- all three commutators `[D_i,D_j]=0`;
- preservation of the Weyl relations;
- preservation of the canonical symplectic form by `widehat F`;
- the exact image-decomposition proof, separately from computational identities.

The first five items are finite exact polynomial certificates.  The image
characterization and injectivity argument should be presented as written
structural proofs rather than inferred from bounded calculations.
