# The master quotient theorem

> **Conditional status.**  Sections 5 and 7 use C22's asserted all-`k`
> Boolean factorization relation.  The conductor-ribbon norm underlying that
> assertion is refuted in
> [C22_CONDUCTOR_RIBBON_AUDIT.md](C22_CONDUCTOR_RIBBON_AUDIT.md).  The
> independent Wronskian and finiteness arguments below remain valid, but the
> completed-collision surjectivity and hence the full closed-immersion theorem
> require a replacement local proof.

This note gives the precise target-side theorem behind the exceptional-seed
construction. Throughout, `k` has characteristic zero and `n>=3`.

## 1. The necessary exact-degree correction

Put

\[
 V_n=k[W]_{\le n},\qquad L=\langle1,W\rangle,
 \qquad \pi:V_n\longrightarrow V_n/L.
\]

The full degree-at-most-`n` no-simple-root locus is too large: all
`(W-a)^2` have no simple root and have the same image modulo `L`. Define
instead the reduced monic exact-degree locus

\[
 B_n=\{M=W^n+m_{n-1}W^{n-1}+\cdots+m_0:
       \text{every root of }M\text{ has multiplicity at least }2\}. \tag{1}
\]

It is the finite union of the monic coincident-root loci
`Delta_lambda`, for partitions `lambda` of `n` with no part one. The
nonmonic exact-degree version is its product with the leading-coefficient
torus, so the monic section loses no quotient information. In coordinates,

\[
 q:=\pi|_{B_n}:B_n\longrightarrow\mathbb A^{n-2},\qquad
 (m_0,m_1,m_2,\ldots,m_{n-1})\longmapsto(m_2,\ldots,m_{n-1}).       \tag{2}
\]

## 2. The theorem

**Theorem (master quotient theorem).**

1. The morphism `q` is a closed immersion. Equivalently, in the reduced
   coordinate ring of `B_n`, the coefficients `m_0,m_1` belong to the
   subalgebra generated by `m_2,...,m_(n-1)`. In particular `q` identifies
   `B_n` scheme-theoretically with its closed image.
2. Define

   \[
   \Phi(M)=\sum_{j=2}^n m_j,\qquad
   D(M)=\sum_{j=2}^n j m_j,\qquad
   A(M)=M''(1)-2D(M),                                               \tag{3}
   \]

   and

   \[
      X_n=B_n\cap V(\Phi)\cap D(D A).                              \tag{4}
   \]

   In the normalized admissible seed space

   \[
   \mathcal S_n=\{H:\deg H=n, H(0)=H'(0)=H(1)=0,
                    H'(1)=-1, H''(1)\ne-2\},                       \tag{5}
   \]

   the exceptional seed locus is exactly

   \[
      \mathcal N_n=\rho(X_n),\qquad
      \rho(M)={-M+M'(0)W+M(0)\over D(M)}.                           \tag{6}
   \]

   With reduced structures, (6) is an isomorphism of `X_n` onto the closed
   exceptional locus `mathcal N_n` in `mathcal S_n`.

All assertions are geometric: they persist after extending the
characteristic-zero ground field.

## 3. The global Wronskian lemma

Every polynomial in `B_n` lies on a maximal `2/3` component, hence has a
factorization

\[
 M=Q^2R^3,\qquad \deg Q=a,\quad\deg R=b,\quad 2a+3b=n,             \tag{7}
\]

with monic factors. The following identity is the decisive point. It is valid
over every commutative `Q`-algebra, so it sees nilpotent collision directions.

**Lemma (global affine rigidity).** Let

\[
 M=Q^2R^3,\qquad N=S^2T^3,
 \qquad 2\deg Q+3\deg R=2\deg S+3\deg T=n.
\]

If `M-N=lambda W+mu`, then `lambda=mu=0`.

**Proof.** Put `a=deg Q,b=deg R,c=deg S,d=deg T` and

\[
 \mathcal W=NM'-MN'=N(M-N)'-(M-N)N'.                              \tag{8}
\]

The derivative factorizations show that `mathcal W` is divisible by the monic
polynomial

\[
 F=QR^2ST^2,
 \qquad \deg F=a+2b+c+2d=n+{b+d\over2}.                           \tag{9}
\]

The degree equations show that `b` and `d` have the same parity. If `b+d>0`, then
`b+d>=2`, so `deg F>=n+1`; the right side of (8) has degree at most `n`.
Multiplication by a monic polynomial preserves degree over an arbitrary
coefficient ring, and therefore `mathcal W=0`. Substitution of
`M-N=lambda W+mu` gives

\[
 N\lambda-N'(\lambda W+\mu)=0.
\]

Its leading coefficient is `(1-n)lambda`, hence `lambda=0`; the leading
coefficient of `-mu N'` is `-n mu`, hence `mu=0`. If `b=d=0`, both
polynomials are pure squares and

\[
 M-N=(Q-S)(Q+S).
\]

The second factor is monic up to the unit `2` and has degree `n/2>=2`, so an
affine product must again be zero. This proves the lemma. `square`

Unlike a pointwise Mason argument, the lemma applies simultaneously to any
two factorization branches over an Artin coefficient algebra. Thus it kills
the two shared affine coefficients before the collision is separated into
local transfer blocks.

## 4. Finiteness

Fix `lambda=(lambda_1,...,lambda_ell)`. Its ordered-root map is

\[
 (r_1,\ldots,r_\ell)\longmapsto
 M_\lambda(W)=\prod_i(W-r_i)^{\lambda_i}.                          \tag{10}
\]

By the weighted Newton identities, the highest `ell` nonleading
coefficients are polynomially equivalent to

\[
 p_j=\sum_i\lambda_i r_i^j,\qquad1\le j\le\ell.                  \tag{11}
\]

These homogeneous forms have no common projective zero. Indeed, group the
nonzero `r_i` by distinct values `u_1,...,u_e`, and let `Lambda_a` be the sum
of the positive integer weights in group `a`. The first `e` equations give

\[
 \sum_a\Lambda_a u_a^j=0,\qquad1\le j\le e.
\]

The matrix `(u_a^j)` is an invertible Vandermonde matrix, forcing every
`Lambda_a=0`, impossible in characteristic zero. Hence all `r_i=0`.
The projective base-locus criterion now makes (11) finite. The same holds
after quotienting permutations of roots with equal weights; that quotient is
the normalization of `Delta_lambda`.

Since `ell<=n/2`, all these highest coefficients have degree at least
`n-ell>=ceil(n/2)>=2`, so they survive (2). The coordinate ring of
`Delta_lambda` is a submodule of its finite normalization and is therefore
finite over its projected coefficient ring. Finally, the reduced coordinate
ring of the finite union `B_n` embeds in the product of its component rings,
so it too is finite over the ring generated by `m_2,...,m_(n-1)`. Thus `q` is
finite.

The Wronskian lemma gives geometric injectivity as a special case. Hence the
finite morphism is radicial and a universal homeomorphism onto its closed
image. The next section upgrades this to a scheme-theoretic isomorphism.

## 5. The completed collision equalizer

Fix a collision polynomial

\[
 M_0=\prod_{\rho=1}^{e}(W-r_\rho)^{m_\rho}.
\]

The formal normalization branches of the maximal `2/3` components through
`M_0` are indexed by allocations

\[
 A=((i_\rho,j_\rho))_\rho,qquad
 2i_\rho+3j_\rho=m_\rho.                                         \tag{12}
\]

For two allocations `A,A'`, write

\[
 i_\rho-i'_\rho=3k_\rho,qquad
 j_\rho-j'_\rho=-2k_\rho.                                       \tag{13}
\]

Formal Hensel factorization and C22 identify their strong completed overlap
with a smooth common factor times

\[
 \widehat\bigotimes_{\rho:k_\rho\ne0} Z_{|k_\rho|}.              \tag{14}
\]

The Boolean model of C22 is the full scheme-theoretic factorization relation,
not its reduction: it retains the square-zero cusp directions at every
ordered root. Taking the products (14), together with their higher collision
compatibilities, reconstructs the completed union. Concretely, order the
finite set of formal branches and induct with the elementary two-ideal
identity

\[
 A/(I\cap J)=A/I\mathop\times_{A/(I+J)}A/J.
\]

Hensel uniqueness identifies every overlap in this induction root by root,
and the Boolean relation supplies its complete nonreduced structure. The
resulting completed equalizer is `\widehat{\mathcal O}_{B_n,M_0}`.

After projection modulo `<1,W>`, the same completed relation is obtained by
replacing strong equality with

\[
 M_A-M_{A'}=\lambda W+\mu,                                      \tag{15}
\]

where the two coefficients are shared by every Hensel cluster. Apply the
global Wronskian lemma in the universal completed coefficient algebra of this
relation. It gives `lambda=mu=0`; consequently (15) and strong equality define
the same formal scheme, at every level of the completed relation. Hence

\[
 \widehat{\mathcal O}_{q(B_n),q(M_0)}
       \longrightarrow \widehat{\mathcal O}_{B_n,M_0}            \tag{16}
\]

is surjective (indeed, an isomorphism).

The map on local rings is finite, and completion is faithfully flat. Its
cokernel therefore vanishes whenever its completed cokernel vanishes. Thus
`O_(q(B_n),q(M_0))->O_(B_n,M_0)` is surjective for every collision point.
The finite map `q` is a closed immersion.

This argument is precisely where the local all-`k` theorem is used. A reduced
normalization relation would miss conductor nilpotents; the Boolean
factorization relation in C22 supplies them, and the Wronskian removes the
only global coupling.

## 6. The exceptional slice

On `Phi=0,D!=0`, formula (6) satisfies

\[
 \rho(M)(0)=\rho(M)'(0)=\rho(M)(1)=0,qquad \rho(M)'(1)=-1.         \tag{12}
\]

Also `rho(M)''(1)=-M''(1)/D(M)`, so the forbidden equation
`rho(M)''(1)=-2` is exactly `A(M)=0`. On the monic chart, `rho` is an
isomorphism to the exact-degree normalized-seed chart: for
`H=sum h_jW^j`, its inverse is

\[
 m_j=h_j/h_n\quad(j\ge2).                                         \tag{13}
\]

If `H` is normalized and admissible, an omitted pencil value `(s,t)` is,
by C07, exactly a polynomial `P=H-sW+t` with no simple root. Its monic
rescaling `M=P/h_n` lies in (4): affine terms cancel from `Phi`,
`D(M)=-1/h_n`, and direct substitution gives `rho(M)=H`. Conversely,
`M in X_n` differs from a nonzero scalar multiple of `rho(M)` by an affine
polynomial and therefore supplies an omitted pencil value. This proves the
set equality in (6).

The functions `Phi,D,A` use only retained coefficients, so (4) is a closed
base change of (2) followed by an open base change. Closed immersions persist
under base change, and `rho` is the displayed chart isomorphism. This proves
the scheme-theoretic assertion in part 2.

## 7. Strength and audit boundary

| Strengthening | Status |
|---|---|
| set-theoretically injective | proved on the monic exact-degree locus |
| finite birational | proved componentwise |
| finite radicial / universal homeomorphism | follows from the closed immersion |
| unramified | proved globally, including collisions |
| locally closed immersion | proved on every exact contact stratum |
| closed immersion | proved globally and on the admissible slice |

No arbitrary-collision affine-rigidity obligation remains. The independent
audit boundary is the formal factorization descent: Hensel splitting must be
used together with the full Boolean relation of C22, rather than with reduced
normalization branches alone.

## External inputs

The quantified proof uses the standard finite-quotient normalization of
coincident-root loci, the projective base-locus criterion for a homogeneous
map to be finite, formal Hensel factorization, faithful flatness of completion,
and C22's Boolean factorization relation. No bounded-degree computation proves
any all-degree assertion here.

For the scheme-theoretic bridge criteria, see the Stacks Project sections on
[completion for Noetherian rings](https://stacks.math.columbia.edu/tag/00M9)
