# Omitted-value classification for arbitrary seeds

This note gives an exact finite algorithm for the remaining image problem and
a closed classification for primitives with two additional simple zeros.

## Complete factor-matching criterion

Let `H in k[W]` have degree `n>=3` over a characteristic-zero field and write

\[
E_{s,t}(W)=H(W)-sW+t.
\]

A value `(s,t)` is omitted by the affine weighted map on `C!=0` exactly when
`E_{s,t}` has no simple root. If its distinct roots have multiplicities
`lambda_1,...,lambda_r`, then every `lambda_i>=2` and

\[
n=\lambda_1+\cdots+\lambda_r.
\]

Conversely, every such multiplicity partition gives an exhaustive
factorization ansatz

\[
E_{s,t}(W)=h_n\prod_{i=1}^r(W-r_i)^{\lambda_i},
\]

where `h_n` is the leading coefficient of `H`. Since subtracting `sW-t`
does not change coefficients of degrees at least two, comparison of the
coefficients `W^{n-1},...,W^2` is a finite polynomial system in the `r_i`.
After solving it, the remaining linear and constant coefficients recover
`s,t` uniquely.

This proves both directions of the classifier:

- every returned factorization has no multiplicity-one root;
- every omitted value occurs in one of the enumerated partitions.

The implementation is `jcsearch.omitted.classify_omitted_values`. It returns
the exact `(s,t)`, multiplicity partition, roots, and factorization certificate.
It avoids solving the full singular locus of the discriminant, whose radical
expressions become unnecessarily large even in inverse degree five.

For a weighted seed with normalization `c`, the affine omitted locus is the
lift

\[
\{(A,B,C):C\ne0,\ (BC,cAC^2)\in\Omega_H\},
\]

where `Omega_H` is the finite output of the classifier.

## Two additional simple zeros

Write their sum and product as `R` and `P`. The normalized primitive is

\[
H(W)=K W^2(1-W)(W^2-RW+P),
\qquad
K={c\over1-R+P},
\]

subject to the usual distinct-root, endpoint, and admissibility exclusions.
The inverse degree is five. A polynomial of degree five without a simple root
has multiplicity type `(3,2)`; the quintuple-root case is included by allowing
the two displayed roots to coincide. Thus an omitted factorization must be

\[
H(W)-sW+t=-K(W-a)^3(W-b)^2.
\]

Matching the three unaffected coefficients gives

\[
R+1=3a+2b,
\]

\[
P+R=3a^2+6ab+b^2,
\]

\[
P=a^3+6a^2b+3ab^2.
\]

Eliminating `a,b` gives one exact condition:

\[
\begin{aligned}
F(R,P)={}&20P^3+219P^2R^2-312P^2R+84P^2\\
&-120PR^4+174PR^3+54PR^2-204PR+96P\\
&+16R^6-24R^5-21R^4+58R^3-21R^2-24R+16.
\end{aligned}
\]

Because the two remaining univariate equations in `a` have nonzero constant
leading coefficients, their resultant vanishes exactly when they share an
affine root. Therefore

\[
\Omega_H\ne\varnothing\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad F(R,P)=0.
\]

For every solution `(a,b)`, the corresponding omitted value is

\[
s=Ka^2b(2a+3b),
\qquad
t=Ka^3b^2.
\]

Thus a generic primitive with two additional simple zeros is surjective. The
exceptional seeds form the explicit coefficient hypersurface `F=0`.

For example,

\[
H(W)={1\over27}W^2(1-W)(W^2+8W+18)
\]

has `(R,P)=(-8,18)`, lies on `F=0`, and has the unique certificate

\[
H(W)+W-1=-{1\over27}(W+3)^3(W-1)^2.
\]

Hence `Omega_H={(-1,-1)}`.

## More and repeated extra zeros

The same algorithm is already complete for any fixed number and multiplicity
of extra zeros. For inverse degree six it checks the four possible types

\[
(6),\quad(4,2),\quad(3,3),\quad(2,2,2).
\]

The executable audit includes generic representatives with two and three
simple extra zeros and a representative with a repeated extra zero. Both
generic simple-root representatives and the repeated-root representative are
surjective. This is a classification algorithm for arbitrary fixed seeds;
compact equations analogous to `F(R,P)` for higher seed-parameter spaces can
be generated by eliminating the factor roots when needed.

The complementary boundary saturation theorem for repeated extra roots is
`REPEATED_ROOT_BOUNDARY.md`.

The dual-curve interpretation of every multiplicity partition, including the
geometric meaning of `F(R,P)=0`, is given in
`GENERIC_DISCRIMINANT_CURVE.md`.
