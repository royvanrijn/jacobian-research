# The master quotient theorem

This note gives the precise target-side theorem behind the exceptional-seed
construction. Throughout, `k` has characteristic zero and `n>=3`.

## 1. The necessary exact-degree correction

Put

\[
 V_n=k[W]_{\le n},\qquad L=\langle1,W\rangle,
 \qquad \pi:V_n\longrightarrow V_n/L.
\]

The full degree-at-most-`n` no-simple-root locus is too large: all
`(W-a)^2` have no simple root and have the same image modulo `L`. Define
instead the reduced monic exact-degree locus

\[
 B_n=\{M=W^n+m_{n-1}W^{n-1}+\cdots+m_0:
       \text{every root of }M\text{ has multiplicity at least }2\}. \tag{1}
\]

It is the finite union of the monic coincident-root loci
`Delta_lambda`, for partitions `lambda` of `n` with no part one. The
nonmonic exact-degree version is its product with the leading-coefficient
torus, so the monic section loses no quotient information. In coordinates,

\[
 q:=\pi|_{B_n}:B_n\longrightarrow\mathbb A^{n-2},\qquad
 (m_0,m_1,m_2,\ldots,m_{n-1})\longmapsto(m_2,\ldots,m_{n-1}).       \tag{2}
\]

## 2. The theorem

**Theorem (master quotient theorem).**

1. The morphism `q` is finite and radicial. It identifies `B_n` with its
   closed image as a universal homeomorphism, and is finite birational on
   every irreducible component.
2. For every partition `lambda`, the restriction of `q` to the locally
   closed stratum `B_lambda^ex` of polynomials with distinct roots and exact
   multiplicities `lambda` is unramified and a locally closed immersion.
3. Define

   \[
   \Phi(M)=\sum_{j=2}^n m_j,\qquad
   D(M)=\sum_{j=2}^n j m_j,\qquad
   A(M)=M''(1)-2D(M),                                               \tag{3}
   \]

   and

   \[
      X_n=B_n\cap V(\Phi)\cap D(D A).                              \tag{4}
   \]

   In the normalized admissible seed space

   \[
   \mathcal S_n=\{H:\deg H=n, H(0)=H'(0)=H(1)=0,
                    H'(1)=-1, H''(1)\ne-2\},                       \tag{5}
   \]

   the exceptional seed locus is exactly

   \[
      \mathcal N_n=\rho(X_n),\qquad
      \rho(M)={-M+M'(0)W+M(0)\over D(M)}.                           \tag{6}
   \]

   With reduced structures, (6) is a finite radicial surjection
   `X_n -> mathcal N_n`, hence a universal homeomorphism which is
   componentwise finite birational and is a locally closed immersion on each
   exact contact stratum.

All assertions are geometric: they persist after extending the
characteristic-zero ground field.

## 3. Set-theoretic rigidity

Let `P,Q in B_n(K)` over an algebraically closed extension `K/k`, with
`q(P)=q(Q)`. Then `P-Q=L_0` has degree at most one. If `L_0` is nonzero,
`P,Q,L_0` are pairwise coprime: a common root of `P,Q` would be a zero of
order at least two of their affine difference, and a common root of either
one with `L_0` would then be common to both.

Write `ell(P),ell(Q)` for the numbers of distinct roots. If `L_0` is linear,
polynomial Mason--Stothers gives

\[
 n\le\ell(P)+\ell(Q).                                              \tag{7}
\]

Both support sizes are at most `n/2`. Equality is forced, `n` is even, and
`P=R^2,Q=S^2` with `deg R=deg S=n/2`. But

\[
 L_0=(R-S)(R+S)
\]

has degree at least `n/2>=2`, a contradiction; for `n=3`, (7) is already
impossible. If `L_0` is constant, Mason gives the stronger impossible bound
`n<=ell(P)+ell(Q)-1`. Thus `P=Q`. The argument works after every algebraically
closed characteristic-zero extension.

## 4. Finiteness

Fix `lambda=(lambda_1,...,lambda_ell)`. Its ordered-root map is

\[
 (r_1,\ldots,r_\ell)\longmapsto
 M_\lambda(W)=\prod_i(W-r_i)^{\lambda_i}.                           \tag{8}
\]

By the weighted Newton identities, the highest `ell` nonleading
coefficients are polynomially equivalent to

\[
 p_j=\sum_i\lambda_i r_i^j,\qquad1\le j\le\ell.                    \tag{9}
\]

These homogeneous forms have no common projective zero. Indeed, group the
nonzero `r_i` by distinct values `u_1,...,u_e`, and let `Lambda_a` be the sum
of the positive integer weights in group `a`. The first `e` equations give

\[
 \sum_a\Lambda_a u_a^j=0,\qquad1\le j\le e.
\]

The matrix `(u_a^j)` is an invertible Vandermonde matrix, forcing every
`Lambda_a=0`, impossible in characteristic zero. Hence all `r_i=0`.
The projective base-locus criterion now makes (9) finite. The same holds
after quotienting permutations of roots with equal weights; that quotient is
the normalization of `Delta_lambda`.

Since `ell<=n/2`, all these highest coefficients have degree at least
`n-ell>=ceil(n/2)>=2`, so they survive (2). The coordinate ring of
`Delta_lambda` is a submodule of its finite normalization and is therefore
finite over its projected coefficient ring. Finally, the reduced coordinate
ring of the finite union `B_n` embeds in the product of its component rings,
so it too is finite over the ring generated by `m_2,...,m_n`. Thus `q` is
finite.

Geometric injectivity plus finiteness makes `q` radicial. A finite radicial
morphism is a universal homeomorphism onto its closed image. Its component
function-field extensions are purely inseparable and hence trivial in
characteristic zero, proving componentwise finite birationality.

## 5. Exact strata are immersed

At a geometric point of `B_lambda^ex`, differentiating (8) gives

\[
 \dot M=-M\sum_i{\lambda_i\dot r_i\over W-r_i}.                    \tag{10}
\]

It is divisible by

\[
 G=\prod_i(W-r_i)^{\lambda_i-1},\qquad
 \deg G=n-\ell\ge2.                                                \tag{11}
\]

If `dq(dot M)=0`, then `dot M` is affine, so (11) forces `dot M=0`;
residues in (10) force every `dot r_i=0`. The quotient by permutations of
equal-weight distinct roots is etale, so the same tangent calculation applies
to the exact stratum. The restriction is unramified and universally
injective. Its image is locally closed because the finite universal
homeomorphism `q` carries locally closed sets to locally closed sets. After
factoring through that image, the map is finite (work in an open where the
stratum is closed), unramified, and universally injective, hence a closed
immersion. Composing with the locally closed image inclusion proves that the
original restriction is a locally closed immersion.

This controls each stratum separately. At a collision, the tangent space of
the whole reduced union can also contain directions from several refining
components; (10) does not control those mixed infinitesimal directions.

## 6. The exceptional slice

On `Phi=0,D!=0`, formula (6) satisfies

\[
 \rho(M)(0)=\rho(M)'(0)=\rho(M)(1)=0,qquad \rho(M)'(1)=-1.         \tag{12}
\]

Also `rho(M)''(1)=-M''(1)/D(M)`, so the forbidden equation
`rho(M)''(1)=-2` is exactly `A(M)=0`. On the monic chart, `rho` is an
isomorphism to the exact-degree normalized-seed chart: for
`H=sum h_jW^j`, its inverse is

\[
 m_j=h_j/h_n\quad(j\ge2).                                         \tag{13}
\]

If `H` is normalized and admissible, an omitted pencil value `(s,t)` is,
by C07, exactly a polynomial `P=H-sW+t` with no simple root. Its monic
rescaling `M=P/h_n` lies in (4): affine terms cancel from `Phi`,
`D(M)=-1/h_n`, and direct substitution gives `rho(M)=H`. Conversely,
`M in X_n` differs from a nonzero scalar multiple of `rho(M)` by an affine
polynomial and therefore supplies an omitted pencil value. This proves the
set equality in (6).

The functions `Phi,D,A` use only retained coefficients, so (4) is a closed
base change of (2) followed by an open base change. Finiteness and
radiciality persist, and the image is closed in the normalized admissible
seed space. Giving it its reduced structure proves part 3.

## 7. Strongest justified form and the remaining obligation

| Strengthening | Status |
|---|---|
| set-theoretically injective | proved on the monic exact-degree locus |
| finite birational | proved componentwise |
| finite radicial / universal homeomorphism | proved globally and on the admissible slice |
| unramified | proved on every exact contact stratum; open for the whole union at collisions |
| locally closed immersion | proved on every exact contact stratum |
| closed immersion on the whole admissible open | open in general |

The last assertion says that, in the reduced coordinate ring of the
admissible no-simple-root locus, `m_0,m_1` belong to the subalgebra generated
by `m_2,...,m_n`. Because the map is already a finite universal
homeomorphism, any defect is scheme-theoretic and supported at collisions: a
conductor, tangent direction, or higher infinitesimal secant.

This is the obligation measured by the affine transfer schemes.  C22 proves
`Z_k^aff=Z_k` for every individual local block; C18 proves two global
compensating equalizers.  The remaining issue is coupling arbitrary blocks by
one global affine difference.  The local theorem is strong evidence for the
closed-immersion upgrade, but is not by itself an all-collision proof.

## External inputs

The quantified proof uses polynomial Mason--Stothers, the standard
finite-quotient normalization of coincident-root loci, the projective
base-locus criterion for a homogeneous map to be finite, and standard
radicial/immersion criteria. No bounded-degree computation proves any
all-degree assertion here.

For the scheme-theoretic bridge criteria, see the Stacks Project sections on
[universally injective morphisms](https://stacks.math.columbia.edu/tag/01S2),
[universal homeomorphisms](https://stacks.math.columbia.edu/tag/04DC), and
[universally injective unramified morphisms](https://stacks.math.columbia.edu/tag/06ND).
