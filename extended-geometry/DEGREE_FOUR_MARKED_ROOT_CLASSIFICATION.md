# Degree-four marked-root classification

## 0. Result and status

Degree four is the first inverse degree in which the repository's two
noncubic mechanisms can be compared inside one finite-cover problem. The
comparison has a clean answer, but only after the universe is made more
precise than “two boundary vertices and one primitive reconstruction
variable.”

This note establishes the following scoped result.

> **Strict quartic dichotomy.** Every degree-four Keller map in the
> straightened elementary marked-root universe defined in Section 1 is a
> weighted quartic or a cancellation map of type `(m,r)=(2,1)`.

The two branches are disjoint, even after stabilization: their canonical
scheme-theoretic boundary-contact indices are respectively `1` or `3` on the
weighted line and `6` on the cancellation branch.

This is an exhaustion theorem for the strict universe, not for all
degree-four Keller maps and not for every object satisfying only the two
phrases in the first paragraph. The missing general implication is the
**suspension extraction and straightening assertion**:

\[
 \begin{gathered}
 \text{two intrinsic target-boundary vertices}\ +
 \text{one primitive root}\cr
 \Longrightarrow
 \text{one of the two straightened vertical chart mechanisms}.
                                                               \tag{0.1}
 \end{gathered}
\]

It is not presently proved. Section 5 gives the exact defect catalogue. No
third quartic Keller skeleton is constructed here.

The finite-multiplicity paper is used only as a source for construction and
stable-invariance theorems. Nothing in that paper is an exhaustion claim,
and this note does not alter it.

## 1. The universe being classified

Work over an algebraically closed characteristic-zero field `k`. A
**straightened elementary quartic marked-root presentation** is a Keller map

\[
 F:\mathbb A^3\longrightarrow\mathbb A^3
\]

with the following data and hypotheses.

1. **Finite marked cover.** The generic inverse is a connected separable
   degree-four extension generated by one primitive element `T`. The source
   is the regular-reconstruction open in the finite normalization, as in
   [Marked-root Keller maps](../MARKED_ROOT_KELLER_MAPS.md).
2. **Complete two-vertex boundary.** The canonical normalization boundary
   has exactly two target images. They are intrinsically ordered as
   `Z_Delta`, the unique vertex receiving generic ramification, and `Z_0`,
   the other vertex. All affine and boundary primes over their generic
   points satisfy the local degree sum, so “two” is an exhaustion statement,
   not chart visibility.
3. **Elementary suspension core.** After polynomial source and target
   changes, the presentation fits a divisor-minimal, coordinate-preserving
   suspension square. Its plane core has one reduced irreducible critical
   curve `E`; the normalization of `E` is smooth rational with at most two
   punctures. The target chart has no additional divisor ledger.
4. **Primitive marking.** If `E` has one puncture, the marked root restricts
   to a coordinate on `E` and the preserved target coordinate restricts to a
   polynomial in it; the core Jacobian has order one along `E`. If `E` has
   two punctures, a primitive unit `Y` and the other marked function have
   valuation vectors
   \[
      v(Y)=(1,-1),\qquad v(s)=(-m,m),\quad m\ge1.       \tag{1.1}
   \]
5. **Straightened vertical realization.** In the one-puncture case the
   vertical chart is a polynomial weighted chart. In the two-puncture case
   it is a divisor-minimal elementary reciprocal boundary link, its marked
   boundary is not contracted, and both affine completions are polynomial
   threefolds.

Items 1 and 2 are intrinsic finite-cover conditions. Items 3 and 4 are the
marked plane-core supply. Item 5 is the threefold straightening condition.
Keeping these layers separate is essential: the first two do not currently
imply the last three.

A compact scope sentence for citation is therefore:

> Classify degree-four Keller maps admitting a connected one-primitive-root,
> divisor-minimal coordinate-preserving suspension whose complete canonical
> boundary has two intrinsic target vertices, whose critical normalization
> is a primitively marked `A^1` or `G_m`, and whose vertical chart is a
> polynomial weighted chart or an elementary reciprocal link.

## 2. Proof of the strict quartic dichotomy

The proof is an assembly of construction-independent reductions already
proved in the repository.

### 2.1 The one-puncture branch

The normalized critical curve is `A^1`. The primitive section marking and
simple critical divisor put the plane core in the simple-section normal form

\[
 (w,q)\longmapsto(q,wq-H(w)).                          \tag{2.1}
\]

The inverse equation is

\[
 H(W)-sW+t=0.                                          \tag{2.2}
\]

Exact inverse degree four makes `deg H=4`. The polynomial vertical-chart
hypothesis is precisely the weighted lift, so this branch is weighted. On
normalizing `H'(1)=-1`, the endpoint conditions give the full one-parameter
family

\[
 \boxed{
 H_\alpha(W)=W^2(W-1)(\alpha W-\alpha-1),
 \qquad \alpha\ne0,1.}                                \tag{2.3}
\]

There are no omitted coefficients: writing

\[
 H=aW^4+bW^3+dW^2
\]

and imposing `H(1)=0`, `H'(1)=-1` gives
`(a,b,d)=(alpha,-1-2alpha,1+alpha)`. The exclusions are exact degree
(`alpha!=0`) and weighted admissibility (`alpha!=1`).

### 2.2 The two-puncture branch

The normalized critical curve is `G_m`. The primitive valuations (1.1)
give, up to units and a target shear,

\[
 D=1-s(Q-Ps)^m.                                        \tag{2.4}
\]

The marked-core normal-form theorem gives a core with Jacobian `-cD^r` and

\[
 T(s,P,Q)=c\int_0^s\{1-v(Q-Pv)^m\}^r\,dv.             \tag{2.5}
\]

The reciprocal-link completion theorem then forces the cancellation source
chart and its unique finite jet. Its inverse degree is

\[
 N=r(m+1)+1.                                           \tag{2.6}
\]

Putting `N=4`, with `m,r>=1`, gives

\[
 \boxed{(m,r)=(2,1).}                                  \tag{2.7}
\]

The normalized parameter polynomial and jet are

\[
 M_{2,1}(q)=q^2-4q+6,
 \qquad h_q(A)=q+(4q-6)A.                              \tag{2.8}
\]

Thus this branch has exactly two geometric parameter-root presentations,
`q=2+sqrt(-2)` and `q=2-sqrt(-2)`. Marked reconstruction-open faithfulness
separates them even under stable polynomial left--right equivalence.

### 2.3 No overlap

For (2.3), direct quartic discriminant expansion gives

\[
 \operatorname{Disc}_W
 \bigl(H_\alpha(W)-BCW+AC^2\bigr)=C^2Q_\alpha(A,B,C)
                                                               \tag{2.9}
\]

and

\[
 Q_\alpha(A,B,0)=
 -(\alpha+1)^2\bigl(4(\alpha+1)A-B^2\bigr).           \tag{2.10}
\]

For `alpha!=-1`, the two target boundary vertices therefore meet in a
reduced conic and the contact index is `mu=1`. At `alpha=-1`, the seed is
the triple-zero specialization, the raw `C`-order is three, and exact
saturation gives a cubic trace, so `mu=3`.

For cancellation type `(2,1)`, the intrinsic intersection is

\[
 k[Q,R]/\bigl(Q^6(2RQ^2-c_0)\bigr)
 \simeq k[Q,R]/(Q^6)\times k[Q,Q^{-1}],                \tag{2.11}
\]

where `c_0` is the fixed nonzero cancellation scalar. Thus `mu=6`.
Scheme-theoretic boundary contact and its nilpotency index are
stable invariants. Hence neither cancellation point is equivalent, even
after adding identity variables, to any member of the weighted quartic
line. This completes the proof inside the universe of Section 1.

## 3. The known quartic catalogue

The resulting presentation catalogue is small enough to write explicitly.

| branch | parameter space | critical normalization | `e_Delta` | second-boundary/contact data |
|---|---|---|---:|---|
| split weighted | `alpha!=0,1,-1` | `A^1` | 2 | generically unramified; `mu=1` |
| triple-zero weighted | `alpha=-1` | `A^1` | 2 | zero-cluster index 2; `mu=3` |
| cancellation | `q^2-4q+6=0` | `G_m` | 2 | unramified second boundary; `mu=6` |

The explicit quartic map in
[Quartic weighted geometry](QUARTIC_WEIGHTED_GEOMETRY.md) is
`alpha=-1/2`. Zhuang's three external examples occupy

\[
 F4a:\alpha=-1,\qquad
 F4b:\alpha=1/2,\qquad
 F4c:\alpha=-2/3.                                     \tag{3.1}
\]

Thus the external examples add no component to the table.

On the squarefree-Hessian locus, the marked Fitting divisor of a weighted
seed has the affine invariant

\[
 I(\alpha)=
 \frac{3(2\alpha+1)^2}{4\alpha^2+4\alpha+3}.          \tag{3.2}
\]

It gives `I(1/2)=2` and `I(-2/3)=3/19`, recovering the exact distinction
between `F4b` and `F4c`. The full normalized cover together with its unique
affine root-one sheet is stronger: on the ordinary boundary-clean open it
recovers `alpha` itself.

This suggests the **known-presentation space**

\[
 \mathcal M_4^{\rm known}=
 \operatorname{Spec}k[\alpha,(\alpha(\alpha-1))^{-1}]
 \;\sqcup\;
 \operatorname{Spec}k[q]/(q^2-4q+6).                  \tag{3.3}
\]

Equation (3.3) is not yet asserted to be a coarse moduli space for all
quartic Keller maps. It is a parameter scheme for the two classified
presentations. The weighted component maps generically injectively to
stable classes.  The stacky selected-root extension through every collision
stratum and its coarse DVR descent are complete by `H2` and `H3`.

## 4. Why the shorter proposed scope is insufficient

The conditions

\[
 \text{degree four} + \text{two target boundary vertices}
 + \text{one primitive reconstruction variable}       \tag{4.1}
\]

belong to the finite normalization. They do not reconstruct a
coordinate-preserving plane core, control the punctures of its critical
normalization, or straighten either vertical chart. In particular, (4.1)
does not imply any of the following statements used in Section 2:

1. the core critical curve is smooth rational with at most two punctures;
2. the primitive function has the valuation vector in (1.1), rather than a
   general Laurent expansion;
3. ramification in the one-place case is reduced;
4. the target chart has a trivial divisor ledger;
5. the source chart is polynomial weighted or an elementary reciprocal
   link.

Consequently the strict theorem must not be shortened by deleting the
suspension and chart clauses.

## 5. Defect catalogue: every current route to an additional class

The following table turns “find a third skeleton” into finite proof
obligations. The candidate rows are plane cores or chart defects; none is a
known polynomial Keller lift.

| defect from Section 1 | quartic candidate | invariant/test | present status |
|---|---|---|---|
| nonreduced one-place ramification | `D=q-h(w)` with core Jacobian `D^3` and `deg h=1`, since `3*1+1=4` | `e_Delta=4` | plane core exists; polynomial divisor-minimal Keller suspension unknown |
| missing primitive `G_m` valuation | `s=Y^{-m}`, `q=pY^{-m}+L(Y)` for a nonmonomial Laurent polynomial `L` | puncture valuations and Newton polygon | core embeddings exist; admissible quartic lift unknown |
| unstraightened polynomial chart | the weighted core (2.1) with an exotic one-boundary completion of `A^3` | chart orientation, affine-modification center, boundary Stein data | this is the remaining weighted lift problem |
| nontrivial target ledger | a core divisor canceled by a target-chart divisor | relative Jacobian `b`-divisor | outside the current dichotomy; no quartic example known |
| nonrational or three-puncture critical normalization | any degree-four core not covered by the `A^1/G_m` lemma | genus and unit rank | not excluded by (4.1) alone |
| incomplete boundary supply | a hidden prime or higher-codimension reconstruction failure | local sums `sum ef=4` and normalization saturation | excluded only when the two-vertex hypothesis is proved complete |

The first row is the sharpest numerical third-skeleton candidate. Degree
four does **not** remove it: an arbitrary-power coordinate-preserving core
has inverse degree `r deg(h)+1`, and `(r,deg h)=(3,1)` is the second
factorization of three besides the reduced weighted case `(1,3)`. What is
missing is a constant-Jacobian threefold suspension, not a plane formula.

The smallest shared-reconstruction-variable two-divisor triangular ansatz
does not add a row: its exact determinant acquires a third divisor unless
one of the two inverse factors is inactive. A genuine new example must
therefore use a nonmultiplicative source chart, a nontrivial target ledger,
or another primitive variable. The last option is outside the one-variable
universe by definition.

## 6. A finite quartic proof program

An unconditional theorem for the shorter scope (4.1) now reduces to the
following checkpoints.

1. **Extract the suspension.** Recover a coordinate-preserving plane core
   and a divisor-minimal square from the canonical finite normalization.
2. **Bound the critical normalization.** Prove from degree four and the two
   complete boundary vertices that the core is rational with at most two
   punctures.
3. **Exclude total ramification.** Show that the `D^3`, linear-section core
   cannot satisfy a polynomial threefold determinant ledger, or construct
   its lift.
4. **Make the primitive marking automatic.** Exclude nonmonomial Laurent
   functions in inverse degree four.
5. **Straighten the vertical chart.** Prove the polynomial chart is weighted
   and the reciprocal chart is the elementary noncontracting link.
6. **Complete moduli faithfulness.** Extend the affine root-one marking over
   the finitely many collision parameters of the weighted line.

A failure at checkpoint 3 or 4 would produce a third core skeleton. A
failure at checkpoint 5 would identify exactly which completion hypothesis
admits additional Keller classes. If all six hold, (3.3), with the required
stack structure at special automorphism points, is the degree-four moduli
object.
