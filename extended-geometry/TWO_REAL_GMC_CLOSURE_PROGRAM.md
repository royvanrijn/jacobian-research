# Closure program for GMC in two real variables

## 1. The finite cubic frontier

Use circular coordinates

\[
Z=(X+iY)/\sqrt2,\qquad W=(X-iY)/\sqrt2,\qquad
\mathbb E(Z^aW^b)=\delta_{a,b}a!.
\]

The proved reductions now leave the following cubic problem.

| cubic stratum | original supports/charts | proved reduction |
|---|---:|---|
| at most two weights | — | one-sided |
| exactly three mixed-sign weights | 27 supports / 72 charts | all excluded |
| exactly four mixed-sign weights | 33 supports / 121 charts | all excluded |
| five weights | 21 supports / 102 charts | 18 supports and 92 charts excluded exactly |
| six weights | 7 supports / 44 charts | open |
| seven weights | 1 support / 8 charts | open |

The remaining four-weight supports are

\[
\begin{aligned}
E_1&=\{-3,-1,1,3\},\\
E_2&=\{-2,-1,0,1\},\\
E_3&=\{-2,-1,1,2\},\\
-E_2&=\{-1,0,1,2\}.
\end{aligned}                                      \tag{1.1}
\]

They comprise 24 nonvanishing charts after the support census.  All four
charts of \(E_1\) are excluded by tenth-moment quotient certificates.  The
other 20 charts on

\[
E_2,\quad E_3,\quad -E_2,
\]

are excluded by rational unit ideals through moment six.  Thus the
four-weight frontier is closed.  The last support is obtained from \(E_2\)
by swapping \(Z,W\).

The exact reductions are implemented in
[`verify_two_real_gmc_frontier.py`](../scripts/verify_two_real_gmc_frontier.py).
The source paper’s dimension-two remark stops at two weights; the finite
cubic reduction here is new relative to that remark
([Long, Section 7](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18186)).

## 2. Completed attack G1: the symmetric support is empty

On the constant/constant \(\pm1\) chart of \(E_1\), normalize

\[
P=W^3+W+aZW^2+cZ+dZ^2W+eZ^3,\qquad ce\ne0.        \tag{2.1}
\]

Every odd moment vanishes by parity.  The second moment is linear in \(e\),
so eliminate \(e\) exactly.  After localizing at \(ce\), the equations

\[
M_4=M_6=M_8=0                                     \tag{2.2}
\]

define a zero-dimensional quotient of vector-space dimension 84.  The
completed certificate avoids the expensive rational FGLM conversion:

1. compute the exact 53-element reduced grevlex basis \(G_{\mathbb Q}\);
2. at \(p=1\,000\,003\), independently compute \(G_p\);
3. verify that the reduction of \(G_{\mathbb Q}\) and \(G_p\) have the same
   leading monomials and zero comparison remainders, giving the same 84
   standard monomials;
4. form the \(84\times84\) matrix of multiplication by \(M_{10}\) in the
   modular quotient;
5. verify that this matrix has rank 84.

The last step proves that its determinant is nonzero modulo a certified good
prime, hence nonzero over \(\mathbb Q\).  Thus \(M_{10}\) is a unit in the
order-eight quotient and this chart is empty.  The same independent
rational/modular calculation succeeds on the constant/radial and
radial/radial representatives.  Swapping \(Z,W\) covers the
radial/constant chart.  In all three representative computations the
rational basis has size 53, the quotient length is 84, and the modular
multiplication rank is 84.  Therefore all four charts of \(E_1\) are empty.
The exact replay is
[`verify_two_real_gmc_symmetric_chart.py`](../scripts/verify_two_real_gmc_symmetric_chart.py).

The good-prime multiplication test remains the production pipeline when a
moment ideal first becomes zero-dimensional but not immediately unit.  A
stored rational Bézout identity is a possible smaller artifact, but is no
longer needed for rigor.

## 3. Completed attack G2: centering closes the other three supports

### The zero-weight orbit \(E_2\)

The zero-weight component is \(b_0+b_1U\).  The first moment gives

\[
b_0+b_1=0,
\]

so it is a scalar multiple of \(1-U\).  Eliminate this coefficient before
forming any Gröbner basis.  This lowers every \(E_2\) chart by one variable
and makes the centered radial factor explicit.  Then use the shape/Bézout
pipeline from Attack G1.

### The balanced orbit \(E_3\)

The outer weights \(\pm2\) have constant radial coefficients, while the
\(\pm1\) components are affine in \(U\).  Normalize the two outer
coefficients first.  This leaves four affine-source coefficients and avoids
the worst chart ordering encountered when a \(\pm1\) coefficient is used for
normalization.

For \(E_2\), the two zero-weight coefficient charts become the same open
locus after centering.  Four exact representatives cover \(E_2\), and
reflection covers \(-E_2\).  For \(E_3\), three representatives cover four
charts by reflection.  All seven localized ideals generated by moments
through order six have exact reduced basis \([1]\) over \(\mathbb Q\).
The replay is
[`verify_two_real_gmc_remaining_four_weight.py`](../scripts/verify_two_real_gmc_remaining_four_weight.py).
Together with Attack G1 this closes all 121 four-weight charts.

## 4. Attack G3: close the five-to-seven-weight cubics

### The exact five-weight census

The five-weight pass is now resumable and separates discovery from proof.
There are 21 supports and 102 ordinary nonvanishing coefficient charts.
Reflection gives 12 support representatives.  Centering

\[
c_0+c_1ZW=c_1(ZW-1)
\]

on every support containing weight zero leaves 35 chart representatives
(33 after also identifying the two self-reflective mixed chart pairs).
Moments are generated directly from zero-weight multinomial compositions.

At \(p=1\,000\,003\), every one of the 35 centered/reflected charts has
unit saturated moment ideal already through order eight.  This is discovery
evidence, not by itself a characteristic-zero exclusion.  Independent
rational saturation promotes 31 of the 35 computed presentations to exact
bases \([1]\).  Using chart reflection on the self-reflective balanced
support, these certificates exclude 92 of the 102 ordinary charts and 18
of the 21 supports.

The suggested support

\[
\{-3,-1,0,1,3\}
\]

is completely excluded: all four centered coefficient charts have exact
rational basis \([1]\) through order eight.  On

\[
\{-2,-1,0,1,2\},
\]

the radial/radial chart and the mixed chart orbit are exact; only the
constant/constant \(\pm1\) chart remains.  The complete unresolved
five-weight list, up to support and chart reflection, is therefore:

1. the constant/constant chart on
   \(\{-2,-1,0,1,2\}\);
2. the two charts with the constant weight-\(-1\) coefficient selected on
   \(\{-3,-1,0,1,2\}\), according as the selected weight-\(+1\)
   coefficient is constant or radial.

The second support represents its reflection
\(\{-2,-1,0,1,3\}\), so these three chart orbits account for the ten
ordinary charts not yet excluded exactly.  Modulo the discovery prime, the
order-six saturated quotients on the last two charts have length 164 and
the balanced chart has length 100; adjoining moments seven and eight makes
each quotient zero.  The missing proof step is a good-reduction certificate
for the corresponding rational order-six bases, or a direct rational unit
certificate.  No finite-moment survivor remains, and no all-order witness
has been found.

The compiler and one-record-per-chart artifacts are
[`verify_two_real_gmc_five_weight.py`](../scripts/verify_two_real_gmc_five_weight.py),
with the proved rational frontier replayed independently by
[`verify_two_real_gmc_five_weight_frontier.py`](../scripts/verify_two_real_gmc_five_weight_frontier.py),
[`two_real_gmc_five_weight_systems.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/two_real_gmc_five_weight_systems.json),
and
[`two_real_gmc_five_weight.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/two_real_gmc_five_weight.json).

### Invariant formulation

Direct enumeration originally left 154 charts with five, six, or seven
weights.  The exact five-weight pass reduces the unclosed part to the three
chart orbits above plus the 52 six- and seven-weight charts.  A better
global formulation uses invariant theory.

Write a cubic in Wick-chaos coordinates.  The coefficient space is a sum of
one-dimensional \(\mathbb G_m\)-weight spaces, with the two radial
coefficients at weights \(-1,0,1\).  One-sided support is exactly the torus
null cone.  Every moment \(\mathbb E(P^m)\) is an invariant polynomial in
these coefficients.

The target statement is

\[
\sqrt{\langle\mathbb E(P),\mathbb E(P^2),\ldots\rangle}
\supseteq I_{\rm null},                            \tag{4.1}
\]

where \(I_{\rm null}\) is generated by the mixed-sign weight-zero coefficient
monomials.  Prove (4.1) by reducing a finite generating set of
\(I_{\rm null}\), or powers of its generators, modulo a finite moment ideal.

This is stronger and more reusable than 154 independent saturations:

- it closes every cubic support simultaneously;
- it explains why pure-moment vanishing forces one-sided support;
- one-sided support gives GMC immediately by weight.

The computational milestone is to determine the least \(M\) for which the
radical of the moments through \(M\) contains the cubic null-cone ideal.
The proof artifact should store explicit reductions or Nullstellensatz
identities for the null-cone generators.

## 5. Attack G4: move from cubics to all degrees

For arbitrary degree,

\[
P=\sum_k Z^{k_+}W^{k_-}A_k(U).
\]

Expectation is the composition of constant-term extraction in the weight
variable with the factorial functional

\[
\mathcal L(U^j)=j!.
\]

The desired all-degree theorem is:

> If \(\mathcal L(\operatorname{CT}P^m)=0\) for every \(m>0\), then the
> nonzero weights of \(P\) lie in an open half-line.

There are three plausible routes.

1. **Prime-asymptotic separation.** Choose an extremal primitive
   zero-weight relation among the support and use moments at large primes to
   isolate its factorial valuation from all competing relations.
2. **Radial generating functions.** Replace
   \(\mathcal L(A(U))\) by
   \(\int_0^\infty A(u)e^{-u}\,du\) formally and derive a differential
   equation for the constant-term resolvent.  Show that the identically-one
   solution forces a one-sided Newton polygon.
3. **Finite invariant generation by degree.** Prove the null-cone
   containment (4.1) uniformly in the radial degrees, using bounds depending
   only on the weight interval.

The cubic computation is the calibration case: it identifies which route
produces certificates small enough to generalize.

## 6. Order of execution

1. Compute the cubic invariant null-cone ideal and seek the finite
   containment (4.1), closing all remaining cubic supports at once.
2. In parallel, run the exact chart compiler on the 154 five-to-seven-weight
   charts and retain only unit or good-prime multiplication certificates.
3. Generalize the successful certificate mechanism to arbitrary radial
   degree.

A negative computational result is useful only when it outputs the residual
positive-dimensional component or a specific null-cone generator not yet
controlled.  The program should never return to undirected coefficient
optimization.

## 7. Remaining dense-cubic census

The five-weight compiler now has all of the previously missing resumability
features: normalized systems are serialized before Gröbner computation,
each chart has its own result record, modular calculations are labeled as
discovery, and support exclusions are counted only after rational
promotion.

The next finite tasks are:

1. certify good reduction of the three order-six quotients of lengths
   \(164,164,100\), then use order-seven/eight multiplication matrices to
   close the last ten ordinary five-weight charts;
2. extend the same centered direct-saturation compiler to the 44 six-weight
   and eight seven-weight charts;
3. promote only exact rational unit ideals or rational-basis/good-prime
   multiplication certificates.
