# Image and Vanishing counterexamples from the essential cubic collision

The repository's 21-variable cubic-homogeneous Keller collision and its
20-variable identity-output slice produce the following direct
counterexamples, without passing through the Gaussian Moment Conjecture:

\[
 \boxed{\neg\operatorname{SIC}(20)},\qquad
 \boxed{\neg\operatorname{GVC}_{\Delta}(40)},\qquad
 \boxed{\neg\operatorname{VC}_{\mathrm{HN},4}^{\mathrm{hom}}(42)}.
\]

Here the first statement is the Special Image Conjecture and the second is the
generalized Vanishing Conjecture for the ordinary Laplacian.  The resulting
40-variable polynomial has degrees two through four; it is Hessian nilpotent
and also violates the nonhomogeneous HN vanishing property.  The final
statement retains homogeneity: a separate 42-variable quartic violates the
classical homogeneous Hessian-nilpotent Vanishing Conjecture.

This improves the earlier 95-to-190 stable-normal-form witnesses in
[STABLE_NORMAL_FORM_AUDIT.md](STABLE_NORMAL_FORM_AUDIT.md).  It also removes
the previously unspecified inverse-coordinate index: the multiplier is the
first coordinate `z_0`, certified directly by the stored collision.  Section
7 also gives an all-order algebraic recurrence for that inverse coordinate.

## 1. Exact cubic input

The [essential BCW artifact](../artifacts/generated-results/essential_bcw_21_counterexample.json)
stores

\[
 V(z)=z+\mathcal H(z),\qquad z\in\mathbb C^{21},                 \tag{1.1}
\]

where `mathcal H` is cubic homogeneous, `det DV=1`, and three distinct
rational points `a,b,c` have one common image.  In zero-based artifact
coordinates,

\[
 (a_0,b_0,c_0)=(0,1,-1).                                      \tag{1.2}
\]

The construction and collision are regenerated by
[`verify_essential_bcw_21_route.py`](../scripts/verify_essential_bcw_21_route.py)
and independently replayed using only the standard library by
[`audit_essential_bcw_21_independent.py`](../scripts/audit_essential_bcw_21_independent.py).

For the contraction formulas put

\[
 H=-\mathcal H,\qquad F(z)=z-H(z)=V(z),\qquad
 p(w,z)=w\mathbin{\cdot}H(z)=-w\mathbin{\cdot}\mathcal H(z).   \tag{1.3}
\]

Thus `p` is a completely explicit quartic with 60 terms.

## 2. The first inverse coordinate is provably infinite

Define Zhao's contraction

\[
 \mathcal E(w^\alpha q(z))=\partial_z^\alpha q(z).             \tag{2.1}
\]

The coefficient form of the Abhyankar--Gurjar identity proved in
[FIXED_GMC_SIC_PROOF.md](FIXED_GMC_SIC_PROOF.md) gives

\[
 \mathcal E(p^m)=0\quad(m\geq1)                               \tag{2.2}
\]

and, for the formal inverse `G=F^{-1}`,

\[
 G_i(z)=\sum_{m\geq0}\frac1{m!}\mathcal E(z_i p^m).           \tag{2.3}
\]

There is no existential ambiguity in the coordinate.  If `G_0` were a
polynomial, the formal identity

\[
 G_0(F(z))=z_0                                                  \tag{2.4}
\]

would be a polynomial identity and hence would hold at every point.  But
`F(a)=F(b)` while `a_0=0` and `b_0=1`, contradicting (2.4).  Therefore `G_0`
is not a polynomial.

Because `H` is cubic homogeneous, the `m`-th term in (2.3) is homogeneous of
degree `2m+1`.  Different values of `m` cannot cancel.  Consequently

\[
 \boxed{\mathcal E(z_0p^m)\ne0\text{ for infinitely many }m}. \tag{2.5}
\]

This collision argument supplies an all-order certificate; computing a long
finite prefix of the inverse is unnecessary.

## 3. Direct failure of the Special Image Conjecture

On `C[w,z]` define the commuting operators

\[
 \Theta_i=w_i-\partial_{z_i}\qquad(0\leq i<21).                \tag{3.1}
\]

Zhao identifies their joint image with the contraction kernel,

\[
 \operatorname{Im}(\Theta_0,\ldots,\Theta_{20})=\ker\mathcal E.\tag{3.2}
\]

Equations (2.2) and (2.5) now say

\[
 p^m\in\operatorname{Im}\Theta\quad(m\geq1),\qquad
 z_0p^m\notin\operatorname{Im}\Theta
 \quad\text{for infinitely many }m.                           \tag{3.3}
\]

Hence this image is not a Mathieu--Zhao subspace.  This first gives an explicit
counterexample to `SIC(21)`; Section 6 descends it further to `SIC(20)`.  Either
one disproves the unrestricted Image Conjecture.  The image/kernel theorem and
the Image Conjecture are due to
[Zhao](https://arxiv.org/abs/0902.0210); the repository proves the inversion
input locally rather than treating it as a black box.

## 4. Direct generalized Vanishing counterexample

Set

\[
 \Lambda=\sum_{i=0}^{20}\partial_{w_i}\partial_{z_i}.          \tag{4.1}
\]

For every polynomial `Q(z)`, expansion of `Lambda^m` and `p^m` gives

\[
 \begin{aligned}
 \Lambda^m(Qp^m)
 &=\sum_{|\alpha|=m}\frac{m!}{\alpha!}
   \partial_z^\alpha\!\left(Q\,\partial_w^\alpha p^m\right)\\
 &=m!\sum_{|\alpha|=m}\frac{m!}{\alpha!}
   \partial_z^\alpha(QH^\alpha)\\
 &=m!\,\mathcal E(Qp^m).                                     \tag{4.2}
 \end{aligned}
\]

Therefore

\[
 \Lambda^m(p^m)=0\quad(m\geq1),\qquad
 \Lambda^m(z_0p^m)\ne0\quad\text{infinitely often}.           \tag{4.3}
\]

This already disproves the generalized constant-coefficient Vanishing
Conjecture with a specified operator, polynomial, and linear multiplier.  The
general second-order formulation is recorded in
[Zhao's constant-coefficient paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/0704.1691).

Now make the invertible complex-linear change

\[
 w_i=\frac{u_i+iv_i}{\sqrt2},\qquad
 z_i=\frac{u_i-iv_i}{\sqrt2}.                                 \tag{4.4}
\]

Then

\[
 \partial_{w_i}\partial_{z_i}
 =\frac12(\partial_{u_i}^2+\partial_{v_i}^2),\qquad
 \Lambda=\frac12\Delta_{42}.                                 \tag{4.5}
\]

The transformed quartic is

\[
 R(u,v)
 =-\frac14(u+iv)\mathbin{\cdot}\mathcal H(u-iv),              \tag{4.6}
\]

and one may clear the irrelevant scalar in the transformed multiplier by
taking

\[
 Q_0(u,v)=u_0-iv_0.                                           \tag{4.7}
\]

Thus the fully expanded 876-term polynomial in the
[generated witness artifact](../artifacts/generated-results/image_vanishing_counterexamples_21_42.json)
satisfies

\[
 \boxed{\Delta^{m}R^m=0\ (m\geq1),\qquad
 \Delta^m(Q_0R^m)\ne0\ \text{for infinitely many }m}.         \tag{4.8}
\]

This is a homogeneous generalized Laplacian-VC counterexample in 42
variables.  Section 6 improves the generalized dimension to 40 after giving
up homogeneity.

## 5. The same quartic defeats the classical HN Vanishing Conjecture

The symmetric lift makes the stronger classical statement elementary.  Put

\[
 f(x,y)=y^TV(x)=x^Ty+y^T\mathcal H(x).                         \tag{5.1}
\]

Up to ordering the two blocks,

\[
 \nabla f(x,y)=\bigl(DV(x)^Ty,V(x)\bigr).                     \tag{5.2}
\]

Its Hessian has block form

\[
 \operatorname{Hess}f=
 \begin{pmatrix} *&DV^T\\ DV&0\end{pmatrix},
 \qquad
 \det\operatorname{Hess}f=(-1)^{21}(\det DV)^2=-1.            \tag{5.3}
\]

It is noninjective: the points `(a,0)`, `(b,0)`, and `(c,0)` have one common
gradient image.

Under

\[
 x=\frac{u+iv}{\sqrt2},\qquad y=\frac{u-iv}{\sqrt2},           \tag{5.4}
\]

equation (5.1) becomes

\[
 f=\frac12(u^Tu+v^Tv)+P(u,v),\qquad
 P(u,v)=\frac14(u-iv)\mathbin{\cdot}\mathcal H(u+iv).         \tag{5.5}
\]

The transformed gradient map is

\[
 (u,v)\longmapsto(u,v)+\nabla P(u,v).                         \tag{5.6}
\]

It remains noninjective because (5.2) and (5.6) differ only by invertible
linear source and target changes.  Its Jacobian determinant is constant and
equals one at the origin, so

\[
 \det(I+\operatorname{Hess}P)=1.                              \tag{5.7}
\]

Since `P` is quartic homogeneous,
`Hess P(ts)=t^2 Hess P(s)`.  Substituting `ts` in (5.7) shows that every
nonconstant coefficient of the characteristic polynomial of `Hess P(s)`
vanishes.  Hence `Hess P` is nilpotent.

The quartics in Sections 4 and 5 are the same up to a sign and an orthogonal
reflection:

\[
 P(u,v)=-R(u,-v).                                              \tag{5.8}
\]

Consequently `Hess R` is nilpotent, and the noninjective map (5.6) is
conjugate to `(u,v)-grad R(u,v)`.  Zhao's quartic theorem identifies Hessian
nilpotency with

\[
 \Delta^mR^m=0\qquad(m\geq1)                                  \tag{5.9}
\]

and its inverse formula says that eventual vanishing of
`Delta^m R^(m+1)` would make `I-grad R` polynomially invertible.  The explicit
collision rules that out.  Therefore

\[
 \boxed{\Delta^mR^{m+1}\ne0\text{ for infinitely many }m}.    \tag{5.10}
\]

Equations (5.9)--(5.10) are a direct counterexample to the classical quartic
Hessian-nilpotent Vanishing Conjecture in dimension 42.  The precise
HN/Laplacian equivalence and quartic VC are in
[Zhao's Hessian-nilpotent paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/math/0409534).

## 6. Identity output and the next dimension question

The 21-variable artifact still has

\[
 \mathcal H_{20}=0,\qquad (a_{20},b_{20},c_{20})=(1,1,1).      \tag{6.1}
\]

Thus `V(q,s)=(G(q,s),s)`, and restriction to `s=1` gives a
noninjective 20-variable Keller map.  The generator checks that its nonlinear
terms have degrees one, two, and three.  It is not cubic homogeneous, so the
degree-separation arguments in Sections 2 and 5 do not descend automatically.

This leaves concrete optimization questions rather than another proved VC
bound:

- find a rank-aware symmetric reduction of the 20-variable slice;
- determine whether a direct generalized Image/Vanishing witness survives
  without cubic degree separation;
- rehomogenize the slice with fewer than 21 essential variables, if possible.

No dimension minimality is claimed for 21 or 42.

## 7. Reproduction and scope

Run

```bash
.venv/bin/python scripts/verify_essential_bcw_21_route.py
python3 scripts/audit_essential_bcw_21_independent.py
.venv/bin/python scripts/generate_image_vanishing_counterexamples.py
```

The last command verifies the source collision and coordinate separation,
expands `p` and `R`, independently checks (5.8) coefficient by coefficient,
and records the 20-dimensional identity-output slice.  The all-order steps
are proofs in this note: finite computation alone cannot certify “infinitely
many `m`.”

Primary credit for the Image and Vanishing frameworks remains with Zhao and
the cited reduction literature.  The use of the repository's essential
21-variable artifact, the named coordinate-zero certificate, and the
21-to-42 witness optimization are repository-derived and have no external
specialist review recorded.
