# Closing the coupled Danielewski gap

> **Quadratic normalization audit (2026-07-23).**  Restoring the three
> residual coefficients of the ambient-linear function \(x\) changes the
> final ledger from 27 to 30 variables.  Exact modular reconstruction over
> \(\mathbb Q\) gives the unit Gröbner basis for the corrected ideal, so the
> full quadratic obstruction remains closed.  See
> [the all-degree audit](DANIELEWSKI_ALL_DEGREE_OBSTRUCTION_AUDIT.md).

The two-dicritical arithmetic construction is now reduced to one explicit
problem.  Put

\[
D=\{cw=x(x^2+1)\},\qquad
B=k[c,x,w]/(cw-x(x^2+1)),
\]

with residue bracket

\[
\{c,x\}=c,\qquad
\{c,w\}=3x^2+1,\qquad
\{x,w\}=w.
\]

A polynomial contraction

\[
(A,F,G)\colon\mathbb A^1_a\times D\longrightarrow\mathbb A^3
\]

must solve

\[
\boxed{
J=
A_a\{F,G\}-F_a\{A,G\}+G_a\{A,F\}=1.
}                                                    \tag{1}
\]

An \(a\)-preserving solution is excluded by the nonzero residue periods.
At least two target coordinates must depend on \(a\).  The
[full quadratic obstruction](DANIELEWSKI_FULL_QUADRATIC_OBSTRUCTION.md)
now excludes all 127 ambient-quadratic support charts.  Every remaining
coupled contraction has ambient degree at least three.

This note records two new structural lemmas and the prioritized attacks
that can close the remaining gap.

## 1. Leading-\(a\)-face dependence

Write

\[
\begin{aligned}
A&=A_r a^r+\text{lower powers},\\
F&=F_s a^s+\text{lower powers},\\
G&=G_t a^t+\text{lower powers},
\end{aligned}
\qquad
A_rF_sG_t\ne0,                                     \tag{2}
\]

and permute the three targets so that \(r>0\).  The coefficient of
\(a^{r+s+t-1}\) in (1) is

\[
T_{r,s,t}=
rA_r\{F_s,G_t\}
-sF_s\{A_r,G_t\}
+tG_t\{A_r,F_s\}.                                  \tag{3}
\]

In the fraction field \(K=\operatorname{Frac}(B)\), set

\[
U=\frac{F_s^r}{A_r^s},
\qquad
V=\frac{G_t^r}{A_r^t}.                             \tag{4}
\]

The logarithmic bracket identity is

\[
\boxed{
T_{r,s,t}
=
\frac{A_rF_sG_t}{r}
\frac{\{U,V\}}{UV}.
}                                                    \tag{5}
\]

Because the right side of (1) has no positive \(a\)-degree, (3) vanishes.
Equation (5) gives

\[
\{U,V\}=0.                                         \tag{6}
\]

The bracket is symplectic on \(K\), so (6) is equivalent to
\(dU\wedge dV=0\).  In characteristic zero, \(U,V\) are algebraically
dependent.

**Leading-face theorem.**  The highest \(a\)-coefficients of every
solution of (1) collapse to one rational Hamiltonian direction.  More
precisely, the two weighted ratios in (4) generate a subfield of
transcendence degree at most one.

Since \(K=k(c,x)\) is rational of transcendence degree two, Lüroth
reduction places the nonconstant ratios in a single rational parameter
after passing to the relative algebraic closure.  This replaces a
three-function leading face by a one-parameter pencil.

## 2. The residue-flux identity

Let \(d_D\) denote the relative differential on \(D\), and define

\[
\eta(a)=A\,d_DF\wedge d_DG.
\]

Modulo exact two-forms on \(D\),

\[
\begin{aligned}
\frac{d}{da}[\eta(a)]
={}&
[A_a\,d_DF\wedge d_DG\\
&-F_a\,d_DA\wedge d_DG
+G_a\,d_DA\wedge d_DF].
\end{aligned}                                      \tag{7}
\]

Using \(d_DH\wedge d_DK=\{H,K\}\omega\), equation (1) becomes

\[
\boxed{
\frac{d}{da}[\eta(a)]=[\omega]
\quad\text{in }H^2_{\rm dR}(D).
}                                                    \tag{8}
\]

Therefore

\[
\boxed{
[\eta(a)]=a[\omega]+\kappa
}                                                    \tag{9}
\]

for a constant class \(\kappa\).

For the cubic \(P=x(x^2+1)\), \(H^2_{\rm dR}(D)\) has dimension two.
Its periods on the two vanishing spheres are generated by

\[
2\pi i\int_\alpha^\beta h(x)\,dx.
\]

Thus every coefficient of \(a^j\) in \([\eta]\) vanishes for \(j\ge2\),
while the coefficient of \(a\) is the fixed nonzero residue class.

The leading-face theorem says the highest coefficient is rank one in the
Poisson sense.  The flux identity says that, one level below, the
nontrivial two-dimensional residue class must reappear.  Their
incompatibility is the main theoretical route to an all-degree
obstruction.

## 3. Full quadratic coefficient ideal

Normalize the source-linear part to \((a,c,w)\), retain the residual
ambient-linear direction \(x\), and allow all ten quadratic ambient
monomials

\[
ac,ax,aw,a^2,c^2,x^2,w^2,cw,cx,xw.
\]

The determinant expansion has 102 source monomials.  Three independent
linear coefficient equations eliminate three of the 33 target
coefficients.  After removing scalar-duplicate equations, the maximal
quadratic system has

\[
\boxed{30\text{ variables and }89\text{ equations}.} \tag{10}
\]

The complete ideal reduces to \(\{1\}\) over each of

\[
\mathbb F_{32003},\qquad
\mathbb F_{32009},\qquad
\mathbb F_{32027}.                                 \tag{11}
\]

The reductions in (11) originally supplied evidence.  Ordering the 30
coefficient variables by residual monomial first and target row second,
then using exact modular reconstruction over \(\mathbb Q\), makes the
characteristic-zero calculation terminate:

\[
\boxed{1\in I_{\mathbb Q}.}                        \tag{12}
\]

Thus the entire ambient-quadratic coefficient scheme is empty over
characteristic zero.

## 4. Prioritized attacks

### Attack A: certified rational closure of (12) -- completed

The decisive optimization was coefficient-variable order:

1. solve the three linear equations;
2. order variables column-major in the three-by-eleven coefficient matrix;
3. run exact modular-over-\(\mathbb Q\) `modGB("slimgb",I,1)`;
4. obtain the reduced basis \(\{1\}\).

This closes degree two without a modular inference.

### Attack B: leading-face descent

Classify the one-parameter pencils forced by (4)--(6), modulo
automorphisms of \(D\) and weighted rescaling.  Insert that classification
into the coefficient of \(a^{r+s+t-2}\).  The target is to prove that the
next face is still Poisson rank one, contradicting the nonzero linear flux
in (9).  Success would exclude (1) at every \(a\)-degree, not merely
ambient degree two.

### Attack C: explicit de Rham reduction

Implement a normal-form map

\[
B\,\omega\longrightarrow H^2_{\rm dR}(D)
\simeq k^2
\]

using the two vanishing-cycle periods.  Apply it coefficientwise to
\(\eta(a)\).  This gives two linear flux equations before any nonlinear
coefficient elimination and should sharply reduce both the 30-variable
quadratic ideal and the leading-face descent.

This attack is now implemented by the
[de Rham compiler](DANIELEWSKI_DE_RHAM_COMPILER.md).  It identifies
\(H^2_{\rm dR}(D)\) with \(k[x]/\partial_x(Pk[x])\), gives a two-coordinate
recurrence for the cubic, and compiles the maximal quadratic flux to five
nonconstant equations.  The remaining work is to combine those equations
with a small pointwise core or with leading-face descent.

### Attack D: boundary-root comparison

Restrict (1) to

\[
D_\alpha=\{c=0,\ x=\alpha\}\simeq\mathbb A^2_{a,w}
\]

for \(\alpha=i,-i\).  The normal bracket contains the nonzero factor
\(P'(\alpha)\).  Compare the two conjugate restriction systems and require
their images to remain distinct divisors.  This attacks the geometric
requirement that the two dicritical components survive, not just the
volume equation.

### Attack E: automorphism and target-gauge quotient

Before any higher-degree search, quotient by:

- constant \(\mathrm{SL}_3\) target changes;
- translations of the three target coordinates;
- the \(c\leftrightarrow w\) symmetry;
- exponentials of the standard Danielewski locally nilpotent derivations;
- affine changes of the suspension coordinate \(a\).

This should turn leading-face pencils into a small collection of normal
forms rather than another raw coefficient atlas.
