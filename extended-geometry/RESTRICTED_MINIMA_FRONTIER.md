# Exact restricted minima after the cubic counterexample

## 1. The four minima

Over characteristic zero, define

\[
\begin{aligned}
r_{\rm cub}
 &=\min\{\operatorname{rank}_{k(x)}JH:
      X+H\text{ is a noninjective cubic-homogeneous Keller map}\},\\
\nu_{\rm cub}
 &=\min\{\nu:(JH)^\nu=0,\ 
      X+H\text{ is such a map}\},\\
\rho_{\rm HN,4}
 &=\min\{\operatorname{rank}_{k(z)}\operatorname{Hess}P:
      P\text{ is a homogeneous quartic HN Vanishing counterexample}\},\\
n_{\rm HN,4}
 &=\min\{n:P\in k[z_1,\ldots,z_n]\text{ is such a counterexample}\}.
\end{aligned}
\]

The repository now records the rigorous intervals

| minimum | lower bound | upper bound | upper witness |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| \(r_{\rm cub}\) | 3 | **17** | 24-variable circuit-level BCW quotient |
| \(\nu_{\rm cub}\) | 3 | **18** | 22-variable circuit-level BCW quotient |
| \(\rho_{\rm HN,4}\) | 3 | **37** | 44-variable circuit cotangent/HN lift |
| \(n_{\rm HN,4}\) | 6 | 42 | 42-variable cotangent/HN lift |

These are intervals for exact minima, not claims that an endpoint is sharp.
The machine-readable ledger is
[`restricted_minima_frontier.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/restricted_minima_frontier.json),
generated by
[`verify_restricted_minima_frontier.py`](../scripts/verify_restricted_minima_frontier.py).
The prioritized theorem and construction attacks, with explicit success and
failure criteria, are in
[`RESTRICTED_MINIMA_ATTACK_PROGRAM.md`](RESTRICTED_MINIMA_ATTACK_PROGRAM.md).

The rank lower bound uses de Bondt--Sun's theorem that a
cubic-homogeneous Keller map with `rank(JH)<=2` is invertible.  The same
theorem applies to cubic gradient maps and gives the Hessian-rank lower bound.
The dimension lower bound uses the known symmetric nilpotent-Jacobian result
through dimension five.

For the index lower bound, `(JH)^2=0` and Euler's identity give
`JH H=0`.  The derivation `D=H dot partial` therefore satisfies `D(H)=0`;
its exponential sends `X` to `X+tH`, so `X+H` is a quasi-translation with
inverse `X-H`.  Thus a noninjective witness has index at least three.

There is an important scope boundary at index three.  The published
index-three result used here is for **power-linear** maps, not for arbitrary
cubic-homogeneous corrections.  Consequently this repository does not claim
`nu_cub>=4`.  The full-class index-three question remains open in this
ledger.

## 2. Circuit changes lower the exact rank and index

The former 21-variable witness has exact profile

\[
 \operatorname{rank}JH=18,\qquad
 \operatorname{rank}(JH)^j=19-j\quad(1\leq j\leq18),
\]

so its generic index is 19 and a deterministic specialization has Jordan type
`(19,1,1)`.

The new search does not conjugate that terminal map.  It changes the
degree-lowering circuit at the start.  Expose the two polynomial gates

\[
 A=w+xy,\qquad Q=s+xy^2
\]

and apply the target shear

\[
 F_2\longmapsto F_2-12Q(2+3A).
\]

On the zero section this cancels the complete circuit term
`12*x*y^2*(2+3*x*y)` in one operation.  The mixed terms containing `w,s`
are retained exactly; an inverse affine jet restores identity linear part.
Sixteen subsequent shared-factor cleanups reach a quadratic--cubic map in 18
variables.  Its cubic-output coefficient rank is eight, so rank-compressed
homogenization first gives dimension 27.

Constant-kernel removal must be iterated.  The first quotient has kernel
dimension four and exposes a new one-dimensional constant kernel; the second
quotient is a fixed point.  The final map is

\[
 F_{22}=X+H:\mathbb A^{22}\longrightarrow\mathbb A^{22}.
\]

Three rational points remain distinct and have one common image.  Direct
polynomial-matrix multiplication gives

\[
 \boxed{(JH)^{17}\ne0,\qquad (JH)^{18}=0}.
\]

In fact `(JH)^17` has six nonzero matrix entries (eight scalar terms), while
every entry of `(JH)^18` is the zero polynomial.  This identity directly
certifies `det(I+JH)=1`; it is not merely inherited from the BCW bridge.

At the exact rational specialization `x_i=i^2+3i+5`, the Jacobian has rank
18.  An exact Singular syzygy computation gives four generically independent
kernel columns, hence generic rank at most 18.  Therefore

\[
 \operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb Q(X)}JH=18,\qquad
 \operatorname{index}(JH)=18.
\]

The specialized power ranks are

\[
18,16,15,14,\ldots,2,1,0,
\]

with Jordan type `(18,2,1,1)`.  The frozen artifact is
[`index_reduced_bcw_22_counterexample.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/index_reduced_bcw_22_counterexample.json).
Its generator is
[`verify_index_reduced_bcw_22_route.py`](../scripts/verify_index_reduced_bcw_22_route.py).
The dependency-free
[`audit_index_reduced_bcw_22_independent.py`](../scripts/audit_index_reduced_bcw_22_independent.py)
parses the final sparse map, checks the rational collision, obtains exact
specialized rank 18, and multiplies the polynomial Jacobian through powers
17 and 18 using only `Fraction` arithmetic.

This proves the new upper bound

\[
 \boxed{\nu_{\rm cub}\leq18}.
\]

This first witness does not improve the rank or the cubic ambient dimension
21.  A second circuit family does improve the rank.  Expose

\[
 A=w+xy,\qquad R=s+xz,\qquad Y=t+y^2
\]

and use two circuit atoms to cancel `3(1+2xy)^2*xz` in the second
component and `4y^2(1+2xy)(2+3xy)` in the third.  After 18 exact cleanup
steps the quadratic--cubic map has dimension 20 and cubic-output rank nine.
Its 30-variable homogenization has successive constant kernels of dimensions
four and two, leaving a fixed-point quotient in dimension 24.

The exact certificate gives

\[
 \boxed{\operatorname{rank}_{\mathbb Q(X)}JH=17},\qquad
 \boxed{(JH)^{17}\ne0,\ (JH)^{18}=0}.
\]

Seven generically independent syzygy columns give rank at most 17, and the
deterministic rational specialization has rank 17.  Its Jordan type is
`(18,1,1,1,1,1,1)`.  The artifact
[`rank_reduced_bcw_24_counterexample.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/rank_reduced_bcw_24_counterexample.json)
is generated by
[`verify_rank_reduced_bcw_24_route.py`](../scripts/verify_rank_reduced_bcw_24_route.py)
and independently checked by
[`audit_rank_reduced_bcw_24_independent.py`](../scripts/audit_rank_reduced_bcw_24_independent.py).
Consequently

\[
 \boxed{r_{\rm cub}\leq17,\qquad \nu_{\rm cub}\leq18}.
\]

The rank-17 witness is the stronger rank endpoint; the 22-variable rank-18
witness is the smaller construction attaining index 18.  Neither supersedes
the 21-variable witness as the smallest known ambient cubic counterexample.

## 3. Circuit search and power-rank objective

[`search_restricted_bcw_circuits.py`](../scripts/search_restricted_bcw_circuits.py)
implements the search representation required by the new frontier:

1. expose polynomial DAG gates by exact triangular source shears;
2. cancel several circuit terms in one target shear polynomial;
3. keep every mixed residual term rather than substituting gates formally;
4. normalize the affine jet back to the identity;
5. continue with exact shared-factor cleanup;
6. score every retained partial circuit by the rank and the complete sampled
   power-rank tuple of `J(F-id)`;
7. only at a terminal state perform rank-compressed homogenization;
8. iterate constant-kernel quotients to a fixed point;
9. score the quotient by cubic rank/index and cotangent-Hessian rank, then
   sample powers after the orthogonal HN change.

The partial key puts maximum residual degree first, followed by

\[
 \bigl(\operatorname{rank}M,\ \operatorname{rank}M^2,\ldots\bigr),
 \qquad M=J(F-\operatorname{id}),
\]

and then the high-degree residual cost and introduced-variable count.
Circuit-family diversity keeps the old monomial route in the beam as a
control.  This is materially different from terminal coordinate compression:
both winning traces begin with polynomial circuit shears, and neither has a
linear conjugacy to the stored 21-variable terminal map built into the
search.

The retained width-eight run has 16 circuit seeds and profiles 41 terminal
traces.  It finds both the 22-variable index witness and the 24-variable
rank-17 winner.  Its JSON record is
[`restricted_bcw_circuit_search.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/restricted_bcw_circuit_search.json).
The expanded search adds the grouped first-coordinate atom
`x^2*(3*y+x*z)`, so the seed family has 32 members.  Terminal selection is a
Pareto archive for cubic rank, cubic sampled index, cotangent-Hessian rank,
quotient dimension, and transformed-Hessian sampled index.  A width-64
low-complexity/power-profile beam evaluates 396 terminal traces.  Its two
nondominated rank/index/Hessian/dimension vectors are

\[
 (17,18,38,23)\quad\hbox{and}\quad(18,18,37,22).
\]

The latter is the `qb+x2s` plan promoted through the frozen exact generator
and independent replay in Section 4.  Its retained discovery record is
[`restricted_bcw_circuit_search_v2_w64.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/restricted_bcw_circuit_search_v2_w64.json).
All finite-field search values remain diagnostics.  Only the frozen
generators and independent replays promote winning profiles to exact
statements.

The next bounded circuit library tests three non-equivalent gate
factorizations:

- `xxs` replaces the first-component quadratic--quadratic split by the
  linear--cubic gates `x` and `x*(3*y+x*z)`;
- `yvyb` replaces `y^2*v*b` by the balanced product `(y*v)*(y*b)`;
- `ayb` and `xvvz` reuse `y*b` and `v*z` across target components.

The depth-28 `xxs` search reaches 244 terminals.  Its unique Pareto point is
`(19,18,38,23)` and its transformed-Hessian sampled index is 35.  A
structural `yvyb` control reaches 29 terminals with Pareto point
`(18,18,38,23)`, again of sampled transformed index 35.  Finally, the
gate-sharing `ayb+yvyb` beam reaches 46 terminals; its two Pareto points are
`(18,18,40,25)` and `(18,18,41,24)`.  Thus none improves a certified endpoint.
These are bounded negative search results, not lower bounds.  Their records
are respectively
[`restricted_bcw_circuit_search_xxs_w32_d28.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/restricted_bcw_circuit_search_xxs_w32_d28.json),
[`restricted_bcw_circuit_search_yvyb_structural_w22.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/restricted_bcw_circuit_search_yvyb_structural_w22.json),
and
[`restricted_bcw_circuit_search_ayb_yvyb_w36.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/restricted_bcw_circuit_search_ayb_yvyb_w36.json).

A second gate-sharing experiment forces `xvvz` and `v2h`, so the exposed
`v*z` gate feeds both the second and third target components.  Structural
and mixed beams reach 47 and 35 terminals.  Both have the single Pareto
vector `(18,18,41,23)`, so this sharing direction also loses to every
certified endpoint.  The records are
[`restricted_bcw_circuit_search_xvvz_v2h_structural_w25.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/restricted_bcw_circuit_search_xvvz_v2h_structural_w25.json)
and
[`restricted_bcw_circuit_search_xvvz_v2h_mixed_w25.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/restricted_bcw_circuit_search_xvvz_v2h_mixed_w25.json).

A combined width-64 beam then enables all nine circuit atoms at once, keeps
256 prebeam profiles at every one of 24 cleanup depths, and scores 140
terminal maps.  Its three Pareto vectors are

\[
 (17,18,38,24),\qquad
 (17,18,39,23),\qquad
 (18,18,37,22).
\]

Thus it finds no profile below Hessian rank 37 and independently rediscovers
the certified `qb+x2s` route.  The explicit bounded record is
[`restricted_bcw_circuit_search_all_w64.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/restricted_bcw_circuit_search_all_w64.json).
This remains a negative search result, not a proof that rank 37 is minimal.

Finally, a local deformation search starts from the frozen rank-37
`qb+x2s` circuit rather than altering a terminal homogeneous coordinate.
Writing `a=xy` and `q=xy^2`, every tested target shear `lambda*q` is removed
exactly by affine-jet normalization.  For the first nontrivial no-new-gate
family `lambda*a*q`, with
`lambda in {-12,-6,-3,-1,1,3,6,12}`, the residual beam reaches 32 terminals.
Every coefficient-family leader has the same complete diagnostic vector
`(18,18,37,22,35)` as the incumbent.  Its record is
[`rank37_gate_perturbation_search.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/rank37_gate_perturbation_search.json).

The neutral shear `-12*a*s` is the sole tested special coefficient that
cancels a monomial step in the frozen cleanup, so it was restarted as an
independent three-atom search instead of being discarded as a replay
failure.  That search reaches 33 terminals and has Pareto vector
`(18,18,41,23)`.  Its record is
[`restricted_bcw_circuit_search_aspert_m12.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/restricted_bcw_circuit_search_aspert_m12.json).
These coefficient screens are again bounded negative evidence, not
minimality proofs.

## 4. Quartic HN consequences

For a cubic correction `H`, the cotangent potential

\[
 p(x,y)=y^T H(x)
\]

has Hessian

\[
 \begin{pmatrix}
 \sum_i y_i\operatorname{Hess}H_i&(JH)^T\\
 JH&0
 \end{pmatrix}.
\]

The existing 21-variable witness retains the best certified ambient bound
`n_HN,4<=42` and has exact generic Hessian rank 38.  A different two-atom
circuit now improves the rank endpoint.  It exposes the `qb` and `x2s`
atoms, reaches a 19-variable quadratic--cubic map of cubic-output rank eight,
homogenizes in dimension 28, and has successive constant kernels of
dimensions five and one.  The resulting 22-variable cubic collision gives a
44-variable homogeneous quartic HN counterexample.

Three good-prime specializations give block ranks

\[
 \operatorname{rank}JH=18,\qquad
 \operatorname{rank}A=16,\qquad
 \operatorname{rank}\operatorname{Hess}(y^TH)=37.
\]

Over the exact rational function field, Singular produces twelve syzygy
generators containing seven generically independent kernel columns.  Their
product with the Hessian is identically zero, giving rank at most 37, while
an exact rational specialization has rank 37.  Thus

\[
 \boxed{\rho_{\rm HN,4}\leq37}.
\]

The frozen artifact is
[`hessian_rank_reduced_bcw_22_counterexample.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/hessian_rank_reduced_bcw_22_counterexample.json),
generated by
[`verify_hessian_rank_reduced_bcw_22_route.py`](../scripts/verify_hessian_rank_reduced_bcw_22_route.py)
and checked independently by
[`audit_hessian_rank_reduced_bcw_22_independent.py`](../scripts/audit_hessian_rank_reduced_bcw_22_independent.py).
The direct cubic certificate also gives generic rank 18 and
`(JH)^17!=0`, `(JH)^18=0`.
After the orthogonal HN change, four deterministic finite-field
specializations have power ranks

\[
 37,34,32,31,\ldots,2,1,0,
\]

and sampled index 35.  As with the other transformed-Hessian profiles, this
is a diagnostic specialization rather than a polynomial-matrix identity.

The earlier index-18 cubic map has sampled cotangent-Hessian rank 38, and
after the orthogonal change its sampled power profile terminates at index 34
instead of 35.  The rank-17 cubic witness similarly retains sampled Hessian
rank 38, but its HN lift has dimension 48 and sampled index 35.
Both sampled indices are search diagnostics only: congruence preserves Hessian
rank but not powers, and zero at one specialization does not prove a
polynomial-matrix power identity.

The HN search therefore keeps two independent terminal objectives:

- minimize exact generic cotangent-Hessian rank and final doubled dimension;
- retain the transformed-Hessian power-rank tuple as a shortlist signal, not
  as a certified upper bound.

Further progress on `rho_HN,4` or `n_HN,4` must change the rank-producing
pairing in the circuit, not merely remove zero or constant-kernel directions.
The exact block-rank decomposition that isolates this pairing is developed in
the [attack program](RESTRICTED_MINIMA_ATTACK_PROGRAM.md): the rank-37 source
has cotangent kernel excess one, while the rank-17 source has excess four.

## 5. Standard restricted questions

The surviving questions can now be stated without reference to one
construction:

1. What is the largest `r>2` for which every cubic-homogeneous Keller map with
   `rank(JH)<=r` is invertible?
2. What is the largest `d>=2` for which every such map with `(JH)^d=0` is
   invertible?  Resolve `d=3` first for the full class, separately from the
   power-linear theorem.
3. Determine the exact values of `rho_HN,4` and `n_HN,4`.
4. Determine whether the two minima are attained by one quartic; minimizing
   rank and minimizing dimension need not select the same witness.
