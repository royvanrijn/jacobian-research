# The cubic Gaussian null-cone theorem

## 1. Correct statement

Let

\[
 Z=(X+iY)/\sqrt2,\qquad W=(X-iY)/\sqrt2,\qquad U=ZW,
\]

so that \(\mathbb E(Z^aW^b)=\delta_{a,b}a!\).  Write a polynomial
of total degree at most three as

\[
 P=\sum_{k=-3}^3 Z^{k_+}W^{k_-}A_k(U),
 \qquad \deg A_k\leq \left\lfloor\frac{3-|k|}{2}\right\rfloor .
\]

Let \(R\) be the polynomial ring in the ten coefficients, with coefficient
weight \(k\) on the \(A_k\)-coordinates, and put

\[
 M_m=\mathbb E(P^m),\qquad I_{10}=(M_1,\ldots,M_{10}).
\]

Let \(J_{\rm null}\) be the torus null-cone ideal: it is generated by the
two weight-zero coefficient coordinates and all coefficient monomials of
total torus weight zero that use both a positive- and a negative-weight
coordinate.

> **Theorem (finite cubic Gaussian null-cone theorem).**
> Over \(\mathbb C\),
> \[
> J_{\rm null}\subseteq\sqrt{I_{10}}.
> \]
> In fact moments through order eight suffice on every support with at most
> six rotational weights; order ten is used only on the full seven-weight
> support.

The radical cannot be omitted.  Thus the literal proposed containment
\(I_{\rm mom}\supseteq J_{\rm null}\), and also
\((M_1,\ldots,M_{10})\supseteq J_{\rm null}\), are false.

## 2. Why literal ideal containment fails

All \(M_m\) are homogeneous of coefficient degree \(m\).  Modulo the two
weight-zero coefficient variables, the degree-two part of the full moment
ideal is one-dimensional, spanned by

\[
\begin{aligned}
M_2={}&
2n_{1,0}p_{1,0}+4n_{1,0}p_{1,1}
+4n_{1,1}p_{1,0}+12n_{1,1}p_{1,1}\\
&+4n_2p_2+12n_3p_3 .
\end{aligned}
\]

Here \(n_{r,j}\) and \(p_{r,j}\) have weights \(-r\) and \(r\).
Each displayed monomial, for example \(n_3p_3\), is a null-cone generator,
but no individual one is a scalar multiple of \(M_2\).  Higher moments have
coefficient degree at least three and cannot alter degree two.  Therefore

\[
 n_3p_3\notin(M_1,M_2,\ldots),
\]

which disproves literal ideal containment for both the finite and infinite
moment ideals.

Set-theoretic containment is the relevant invariant statement: vanishing of
the moments should put the coefficient point in the null cone.  By the
Nullstellensatz, that statement is exactly
\(J_{\rm null}\subseteq\sqrt{I_{\rm mom}}\).

## 3. Exact finite proof

The supports of a cubic lie in \(\{-3,-2,-1,0,1,2,3\}\).  The existing exact
frontier calculations exclude every mixed-sign support of size at most four
and 92 of the 102 ordinary five-weight coefficient charts.  The new
characteristic-zero calculations are:

| stratum | reduced presentations | ordinary charts covered | cutoff | result over \(\mathbb Q\) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| finite two-weight audit | 11 | all mixed-sign two-weight charts | 10 | \([1]\) on every normalization |
| remaining five-weight charts | 3 | 10 | 8 | \([1]\) on every localization |
| all six-weight charts | 14 | 44 | 8 | \([1]\) on every localization |
| full seven-weight support | 3 | 8 | 10 | \([1]\) on every localization |

The reductions used in the table are exact:

1. \(M_1=0\) writes the weight-zero component as \(h_0(U-1)\).
2. Reflection \(Z\leftrightarrow W\) identifies opposite supports and
   exchanges the two mixed charts on the self-reflective full support.
3. On a coefficient chart, two selected coefficients of distinct torus
   weights are normalized to one.  This uses scalar multiplication of \(P\)
   together with the rotation torus.  Every complex point of the chart meets
   the normalized slice.
4. If \(F\) is the product of the remaining selected coefficients, adjoining
   \(\rho F-1\) is the exact Rabinowitsch presentation of the chart
   localization.
5. `msolve` computes over characteristic zero.  Every system in the table has
   the literal reduced grevlex basis \([1]\); no finite-field promotion is
   being used.

The replay and the input hashes for all 31 finite systems are produced by
[`verify_cubic_gaussian_null_cone_closure.py`](../scripts/verify_cubic_gaussian_null_cone_closure.py).
Its machine-readable record is
[`cubic_gaussian_null_cone_closure.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/cubic_gaussian_null_cone_closure.json).

These chart certificates exclude every coefficient point with at least one
positive and one negative rotational weight.  On the remaining one-sided
locus, \(M_1=M_2=0\) also kills the weight-zero component: after
\(M_1=0\) it is \(h_0(U-1)\), whose second moment is \(h_0^2\).
Consequently \(V(I_{10})\) is contained in the torus null cone, proving the
theorem.

## 4. Consequence for GMC

If the pure moments of a cubic vanish, the theorem gives strictly positive
or strictly negative rotational support.  For a fixed polynomial \(Q\), the
weights of \(Q\) are bounded, whereas every weight in \(P^m\) has magnitude
at least \(m\) and a fixed sign.  Hence \(QP^m\) has no weight-zero term for
all sufficiently large \(m\), so

\[
\mathbb E(QP^m)=0\qquad(m\gg0).
\]

Therefore the Gaussian Moments Conjecture holds for every polynomial of
total degree at most three in two real Gaussian variables.

This does not settle \(\mathrm{GMC}(2)\) in all degrees.  It raises the degree
of any possible two-real-variable counterexample from three to at least four.
