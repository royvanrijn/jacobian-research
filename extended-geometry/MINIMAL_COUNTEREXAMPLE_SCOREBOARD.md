# Minimal counterexample scoreboard

## 1. Purpose

The numbers \(21,20,40,42\) are certified witness sizes, not presumed
endpoints.  This ledger pairs every upper witness with the strongest lower
bound currently justified in the repository and keeps ambient dimension,
rank, nilpotency index, and degree separate.

The machine-readable version is
[`minimal_counterexample_scoreboard.json`](../artifacts/generated-results/minimal_counterexample_scoreboard.json),
generated by
[`verify_minimal_counterexample_scoreboard.py`](../scripts/verify_minimal_counterexample_scoreboard.py).

## 2. Ambient dimensions

| problem | rigorous lower bound | certified upper bound | current interval |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| arbitrary noninvertible Keller map | 2 | 3 | \(2\le n_{\rm Kel}\le3\) |
| cubic-homogeneous noninvertible Keller map | 3 | 21 | \(3\le n_{\rm cub}\le21\) |
| GMC failure, real Gaussian dimension | 2 | 3 | \(2\le n_{\rm GMC}\le3\) |
| SIC failure, pair dimension | 2 | 20 | \(2\le n_{\rm SIC}\le20\) |
| ordinary-Laplacian generalized VC failure | 2 | 40 | \(2\le n_{\Delta{\rm GVC}}\le40\) |
| homogeneous quartic HN-VC failure | 6 | 42 | \(6\le n_{\rm HN,4}\le42\) |

The reasons are deliberately asymmetric:

- one-variable Keller maps are affine, while the foundational collision is
  three-dimensional;
- in ambient dimension at most two a cubic-homogeneous correction has
  Jacobian rank at most two, so the de Bondt--Sun theorem makes the map
  invertible;
- GMC(1) is proved and Long gives an explicit GMC(3) counterexample;
- the one-pair Image Conjecture is proved, while the repository's
  identity-output slice fails SIC(20);
- for the ordinary one-variable Laplacian, the hypothesis at \(m=1\) gives
  \(P''=0\); then
  \(\partial^{2m}(QP^m)=0\) once \(m>\deg Q\);
- the symmetric homogeneous nilpotent-Jacobian result through dimension five
  gives the quartic HN lower endpoint.

Thus GMC has the sharpest remaining ambient question:

\[
\boxed{n_{\rm GMC}\in\{2,3\}.}                     \tag{2.1}
\]

The companion [two-real frontier](TWO_REAL_GMC_FRONTIER.md) now proves that a
dimension-two witness, if it exists, must be at least cubic, use at least
three rotational weights, and be nonlinear in both circular coordinates.

## 3. Restricted rank and index

For noninjective cubic-homogeneous Keller maps \(X+H\), and their homogeneous
quartic HN lifts, the current exact intervals are

| invariant | lower | upper | upper witness |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| generic rank \(\operatorname{rank}JH\) | 3 | 17 | 24-variable circuit quotient |
| polynomial nilpotency index of \(JH\) | 3 | 18 | 22-variable circuit quotient |
| generic rank of the quartic Hessian | 3 | 37 | 44-variable rank-directed HN lift |

The rank lower bound is the rank-two cubic theorem.  The index lower bound is
elementary: if \((JH)^2=0\), then Euler's identity gives \(JH\,H=0\), so
\(X+H\) is the quasi-translation with inverse \(X-H\).  No full-class
index-three invertibility theorem is claimed; the known power-linear and
symmetric-Jacobian results are narrower.  A uniform inverse-degree-nine
strengthening is impossible:
van den Essen's dimension-five generic-rank-three automorphism has
\((JH)^3=0\) and inverse degree thirteen.

The upper endpoints are exact invariants of stored witnesses, not sampled
matrix ranks.  Their construction and independent replays are documented in
the [restricted-minima frontier](RESTRICTED_MINIMA_FRONTIER.md).

## 4. Two exact degree minima

Two degree questions are already closed even though their dimension questions
are not.

### General Keller maps

Wang's theorem proves invertibility in degree at most two.  The stored
cubic-homogeneous collision supplies degree three.  Therefore

\[
\boxed{\min\deg(\text{Keller counterexample})=3.}   \tag{4.1}
\]

### Gaussian moments

The quadratic-Gaussian theorem in
[TWO_REAL_GMC_FRONTIER.md](TWO_REAL_GMC_FRONTIER.md) proves GMC for every
polynomial of degree at most two in every dimension.  Long's explicit
four-real-variable counterexample is cubic.  Therefore

\[
\boxed{\min\deg(\text{GMC counterexample})=3.}      \tag{4.2}
\]

This is independent of the unresolved minimum Gaussian dimension.

## 6. Search implications

The scoreboard separates four genuinely different optimization targets.

1. Lowering the 21-variable cubic witness requires a new stable circuit or a
   nonlinear collision-preserving quotient; terminal linear compression is
   exhausted for the stored map.
2. Lowering rank below 17 or index below 18 requires changing the
   degree-lowering circuit before homogenization.
3. Lowering SIC/GVC from \(20/40\) requires more than another linear identity
   output: the restricted 20-variable correction has full output span.
4. Lowering the homogeneous HN dimension from 42 requires a smaller
   homogeneous cubic source or a different symmetric lift; the 20-variable
   identity slice loses homogeneity.

No failed beam search changes a lower endpoint.  A new upper endpoint is
promoted only after an exact characteristic-zero generator, collision replay,
and invariant certificate.
