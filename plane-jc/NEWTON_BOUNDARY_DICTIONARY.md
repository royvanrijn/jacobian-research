# Newton polygons and boundary geometry

This dictionary compares two languages; it does not identify the GGHV
construction with the repository's marked-point compactification.  The status
column is deliberately conservative.

| Newton / approximate-root term | Boundary-language translation | Status | Qualification |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| primitive direction \((\rho,\sigma)\) and weight \(v_{\rho,\sigma}\) | monomial divisorial valuation associated with the corresponding toric boundary ray | exact for the toric compactification determined by the Newton fan; standard in the literature | A valuation on a later resolved compactification may require further blowups and need not remain a single monomial weight in the original coordinates. |
| Newton edge normal to \((\rho,\sigma)\) | initial form along the boundary divisor of that valuation | exact in the toric model; standard | The edge polynomial records the divisor's leading restriction, not the whole formal neighborhood. |
| possible lower corner | candidate endpoint data for adjacent boundary valuations/components | repository interpretation | A corner is lattice/weight data.  It is not by itself a boundary component or an intersection matrix. |
| regular corner and predecessor/successor direction | transition between adjacent valuation sectors in the Newton fan | standard-to-interpretive | The ordered direction change is exact combinatorially; identifying it with a specific sequence in a minimal log resolution needs a chosen compactification. |
| triangular Laurent transformation \(y\mapsto y+\lambda x^{-q}\) | change of local parameter at infinity, often following a blowup chart | standard analogy | It is an algebra automorphism of the Laurent chart used by GGHV, not literally a blowup morphism of the repository's compactification. |
| approximate root | successive extraction of a local coordinate whose leading valuation is a prescribed multiple | standard in plane-curve/valuation theory | “Approximate root” carries multiplicity and expansion data stronger than merely choosing any local coordinate. |
| admissible complete chain | combinatorial sequence of corner/edge transitions compatible with successive valuation extraction | repository interpretation | It is plausible boundary-blowup data, but the paper constructs it algebraically in Laurent rings; no canonical isomorphism with a dual resolution graph is proved here. |
| final corner / last lower corner | terminal valuation data at the end of the lower Newton process | standard-to-interpretive | Terminal for this algorithm does not necessarily mean the last component of a global compactification. |
| dicritical edge/component | boundary divisor on which the polynomial map is nonconstant, hence maps dominantly to a target-boundary component | standard in the plane polynomial-map literature | This is the strongest entry in the dictionary; residue degree and ramification still require extra data beyond the edge. |
| edge factor and its multiplicity | intersection/contact multiplicity of the leading boundary restriction | standard | Several distinct factors can lie on the same divisor and lead to different subsequent valuations. |
| \((m,n)\)-pair | fixed ratio of leading pole orders of the two coordinate functions | exact at the selected valuations | It does not mean the global coordinate degrees have been independently normalized to \(m,n\); they are common multiples determined by the first corner. |
| coefficient compatibility equation | condition that adjacent valuation layers glue to a Keller pair | repository interpretation | These equations may encode more than intersection data, including residue coefficients and higher jets. |
| unit-ideal / membership contradiction | impossible residue-coefficient and higher-valuation data | exact algebraically; geometric meaning interpretive | Calling it an “impossible intersection pattern” would be too strong without a geometric derivation of the certificate. |
| degree-five coefficient field | finite residue/moduli field generated by normalized leading coefficients | repository interpretation | It is a number field in the universal exact computation, not the residue field of a geometric divisor over the original algebraically closed base. |

## Comparison with the marked-point obstruction

| Repository marked-point obstruction | Newton degree-frontier work |
| --- | --- |
| Excludes the complete source architecture \(U_2(H)=(\mathbb P^1\times\mathbb P^1)\setminus(R_2\cup E_H)\) | Restricts arbitrary hypothetical plane counterexamples after the published minimal/standard normalization |
| Uses units, Picard groups, and boundary divisor classes | Uses Newton polygons, corners, valuations, Laurent transformations, and approximate roots |
| Degree-independent | Produces a numerical lower bound for the larger coordinate degree |
| Conceptual obstruction visible from a two-by-two boundary lattice | Detailed classification at infinity followed by coefficient elimination |

The combined conclusion is only:

> Any hypothetical JC(2) counterexample must avoid the
> marked-point/hyperplane-complement mechanism and must also lie beyond the
> current degree frontier.

The filters are logically independent.  Their conjunction does not prove
JC(2), and the Newton certificate does not strengthen the Picard/unit theorem:
it applies to arbitrary normalized candidates but uses degree-specific data,
whereas the boundary theorem eliminates one architecture in every degree.

## Boundary-valuation research tests

The source-side gluing test for `(72,108)` is complete.  The three adapted
base ideals compile into one source graph, the Wronskian selects the actual
terminal cluster, and both terminal cases give complete 23-component
packages.  They pass the intrinsic `A^2` gates.  This proves that a source
boundary tree, even with `Q,k,p`, is not a strong enough classification
object.

The open tests must use a map-decorated package:

1. Recover the omitted lower Case-1 bands before running the general
   polynomial-right-component sieve on its alternate-chart residue.  The
   archive stops at `P:z^-5,Q:z^-4`, while the polygons continue to
   `P:z^-8,Q:z^-12`; guessed zero coefficients are not valid.  The same
   sieve already excludes Case-2 cover degrees `2,4`.
2. Retire the Case-2 target-resolution branch.  Its forced endpoint
   `G_12 != 0` is incompatible with the seven residual `(J1)` equations:
   their localization is the unit ideal before `(J0)`.
3. Add the three-section boundary linear series only to Case-1 survivors.
   For reference, on the retired Case-2 degree-one gcd row,
   the forced Pluecker weights are `3` at the affine critical point and `13`
   at infinity, leaving exactly `14`.  At the opposite end, an exact
   four-equation unit certificate excludes gcd degree `7`, leaving degrees
   `1,...,6`.
4. Compute the localized second Chern class of the logarithmic differential
   from its generic and nodal Smith profiles.  This is the next invariant
   after the determinant divisor.
5. Search for a determinant or localized-Chern identity whose expansion is
   the hard membership certificate.  No such identity is currently known.
